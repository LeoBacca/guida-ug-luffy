For the **list** I chose to run at the beginning of this **meta**, I decided to take a very generic approach.

For now, I did not feel like I wanted to play any specific **Events** or overly situational cards to focus on particular **matchups**. Those cards will be covered later in the **tech card** section, but for the starting point of the **format**, I wanted my **list** to be as generic as possible.

**Luffy** is a very aggressive **deck**, but at the same time it generates a lot of value. For this reason, the main focus of this **deck** will be **value generation**, while still developing a strong **board** that allows us to pressure and **aggro** our opponent.

There are many ways to recharge our **hand**, which allows us to keep up even in longer games. One of the most important traits of this **deck** is exactly that: we generate value very easily. Almost every card can cycle itself in some way, and if our opponent decides to chase our **board**, we can keep generating resources while maintaining a form of sustained **aggro** over time.

This allows us to slowly reduce both our opponent's **hand size** and **life**, while constantly forcing them to answer our threats.

## Decklist

![Decklist grid](img/ch3_decklist_grid.png)

I think it will still be difficult to find the final **decklist** for **Blue/Green Luffy** in this **format**. The more information we get about the **meta**, the easier it will be to decide which **tech cards** are actually worth playing.

In my initial **list**, I decided to run **You Can Be My Samurai**. This is a card that can really shine in this **deck**, because we develop so many bodies that we often do not care if we have rested characters on the **board**.

Since **Luffy** gives us the possibility to re-stand two **DON!!** every turn, we will often have those extra **DON!!** available to play **You Can Be My Samurai** on two developed bodies and generate a lot of value.

For now, I feel comfortable with this card. I do not think it is mandatory in the **list**, but at the moment I believe it is the most generic option: it is consistent, flexible, and synergizes very well with what the **deck** wants to do.

The way I am currently playing the **deck** is very aggressive, while steadily developing **board** every turn. For this reason, I usually do not care too much if my opponent tries to trade into my smaller characters, because most of them have already cycled themselves.

**You Can Be My Samurai** gives me even more value when I decide to give away my **board**, because it allows me to convert rested bodies into extra resources.

Of course, this also depends on the **matchup**, but overall I think this card is strong(?) into basically every relevant **matchup** in the **meta**.

## 3.1 Card by card explanation

### Monkey D. Luffy

**Luffy** is obviously one of the best cards in the **deck**.

This is one of our best resources to find the missing pieces for our **board development**, while also being a **swinger** most of the time. The card itself is extremely powerful because it can search almost every card in the **deck**, and it can even find another copy of itself.

This makes **Luffy** especially strong when going first. It is one of the best cards to open with, because it gives the **deck** a lot of consistency without any real downside.

It is also one of the best cards to hold in **hand** for the endgame. When we end up having four characters with different names on the **board**, **Luffy** can become the fifth one for only one **DON!!**, allowing us to enable our **re-stand Event** and go for **lethal**.

Of course, **Luffy** is also one of the best openers in the early game. When going first, you can play two copies of him with only one **DON!!**, because your **Leader** allows you to re-stand that **DON!!**. He also fits very well when going second, since you can play a 2-cost **character**, re-stand the **DON!!**, play **Luffy**, and then set up the **You Can Be My Samurai** combo.

For all these reasons, I decided to include four copies. The card is simply too strong, too consistent, and has no real downside. On top of that, once you have four different characters on the **board**, **Luffy** can also swing for **5k**, which makes him relevant even outside of his search effect.

### Mr. 1 Daz Bonez

**Mr.1** is, of course, another staple card in the **deck**, together with **Mr.2**. These two cards are part of the core of the **deck**.

These **2-cost characters** can basically swing for **4k**(?) whenever you attach one **DON!!** to them, while also cycling themselves when played. The card itself is not necessarily super powerful on its own, but it gives a very solid base to the **deck** and adds consistency to our **draw engine**.

**Mr.1** is usually one of the best characters to open with, especially when going second. He becomes slightly worse in the late game, but in the early turns he is one of our strongest openers, also because he is a very good target for **You Can Be My Samurai**.

The card is also very useful because we can always bounce it back with our **4-cost Crocodile**, allowing us to reuse its value later in the game.

When going second, opening with **Mr.1** is one of the best possible starts. You can play him, re-stand the **DON!!** with your **Leader effect**, and then play another copy of **Mr.1** or **Bon Clay**.

What makes this card powerful is that you can simply **aggro** with it. Even if your opponent decides to remove it, you usually do not care too much, because **Mr.1** has already replaced itself by cycling a card. On top of that, it either forced a card out of your opponent's **hand**, dealt one **life**, or absorbed an attack that would have gone somewhere else.

For all these reasons, **Mr.1** is an easy four-of in the **decklist**.

### Mr. 2 Bon Kurei

Together with **Mr.1**, **Mr.2** is one of the best cards to build a solid foundation for our **deck**.

He is one of the best early-game targets, especially when going second, which is usually our preferred curve. In the early game, we want to develop **2-cost characters**, and **Mr.2** fits this plan perfectly.

Compared to **Mr.1**, **Mr.2** is slightly more powerful. The downside is that he does not have **counter**, but his effect is extremely strong against **Leaders** with more than **5k power**, because he can simply copy their power.

This is especially relevant in **matchups** like Nami. After Nami uses her **Leader effect**, **Mr.2** can basically become an **8k attacker** with one **DON!!** attached. Against **8k Leaders**, he can also swing for **7k** with just one **DON!!**, which makes his raw power very efficient.

The same principle we mentioned for **Mr.1** applies here as well: we want to develop **Mr.2**, apply pressure, and force our opponent to answer him. I usually prefer developing **Mr.2** over **Mr.1** most of the time, because he has no **counter**, so I want to get him out of my **hand** as soon as possible.

He is also one of the best targets to bounce back with **4-cost Crocodile**, allowing us to reuse his value later in the game.

In the late game, **Mr.2** scales much better than **Mr.1**, because he does not have the same hand-size limitation. For all these reasons, he is another staple card in the **deck**, giving consistency and power to our early game.

### Emporio Ivankov

**Ivankov** is still one of the more controversial cards I am playing in the **deck**.

From the feedback I gathered from other people testing **Blue/Green Luffy**, I noticed that many players do not really like this card. Personally, however, I think **Ivankov** is almost mandatory.

The main reason is that he gives a lot of consistency to our **4 DON!! turn**. He is one of the best ways to develop **board** in the early game, even though he is not a huge **swinger**, since he is only a **4k character**. That being said, if we compare him to **Mr.1** and **Mr.2**, the main difference is often just that **Ivankov** swings for **5k** instead of **6k** when we attach one **DON!!**.

The best part about this card is that he can basically cheat out resources. For **4 DON!!**, we can develop two bodies and advance our gameplan of having five characters with different names on the **board**.

He also gives a lot of consistency to the **deck**, because together with **1-cost Luffy**, he is one of our best **searchers**. In many situations, you do not even need to search the card you want to play directly off **Ivankov**. Most of the time, you can search for something you will need later, and then simply play another card from your **hand**.

I think the strongest part of **Ivankov** is how powerful he becomes in the late game. He is one of the best resources to reach five characters with different names on the **board** in the same turn.

For example, when you already have three characters with different names, you can play **Ivankov**, play another missing character from his effect or from your **hand**, re-stand two **DON!!** with your **Leader effect**, and then play the **re-stand Event**. This makes **Ivankov**(?) one of the best cards to set up unexpected **lethal** turns in the late game.

For now, I think this card is a four-of. It might eventually go down from three copies, but since it also has **counter**, I honestly do not see a strong reason to cut it.

Overall, I still consider **Ivankov** a staple in the **deck**.

### Crocodile

**Crocodile** is one of my favorite cards in the **deck**.

I have noticed that a lot of people try to cut this card from the **list**, but to be honest, the more you play the **deck**, the more you realize that **Crocodile** is one of the best resources available.

He can be both a very defensive card and a very offensive one. Usually, the turn you develop **Crocodile**, he is going to block a swing. What I am also noticing in this **meta** is that a lot of **decks** like to play on curve, so most of the time **Crocodile** will simply block a **5k swing** for free.

The best thing about this card is that he allows you to bounce back one of your **2-cost characters**, like **Mr.1** or **Mr.2**.

One of the most basic combos, especially on the **5 DON!! turn**, is to attach one **DON!!** to either **Mr.1** or **Mr.2**, swing with it, then play **Crocodile**, bounce that character back, and replay the same character standing. This makes it harder for your opponent to trade into it, while also letting you draw another card.

After that, you can re-stand two **DON!!** with your **Leader effect** and either play another **2-cost character**, play a **1-cost like Luffy**, or sometimes set up **You Can Be My Samurai** by resting **Luffy** and **Crocodile**.

This card can create a lot of interesting combos, and especially in the late game, it can help you generate a lot of resources while putting a very annoying **Blocker** on the **board**.

I do not think there is really any downside to this card. I tried playing three copies at first, but for now I am playing four copies and I feel confident with this ratio.

Overall, I think **Crocodile** is very good in every stage of the game, especially when played on curve.

### Mr. 3 Galdino

**Mr.3**, also known as **Galdino**, is probably one of the best cards printed in **OP16**.

It is one of the most generic and powerful cards they have ever printed for the **blue** color. This card is a powerhouse in basically every **blue deck**, and from what I am noticing, it is going to be played in almost every **blue list** from now on.

I think this card is simply insane.

Especially in the late game, when paired with **7-cost Boa Hancock**, **Mr.3** can completely shut down entire **boards**. In a **deck** like **Blue/Green Luffy**, where we can re-stand two **DON!!** with our **Leader effect**, we are basically only paying two real **DON!!** to activate this card.

The fact that **Mr.3** also draws two cards is incredible. In our **list**, it also combos very well with **You Can Be My Samurai**.

One of the most interesting basic combos I had in many games was playing **7-cost Boa Hancock**, then re-standing two **DON!!** with **Leader effect** and playing **Mr.3**. This means we effectively used nine(?) **DON!!** in total. After that, we can play **You Can Be My Samurai** by resting both **Boa Hancock** and **Mr.3**, then re-standing another character and draw two cards.

In the end, this line allows us to draw four cards and freeze two characters, which is an enormous amount of value.

I think **Mr.3** is a mandatory four-of. The card is simply too strong.

### Buggy

**Buggy** is another insane card that got printed in **OP16**.

This card was clearly not made specifically for this **deck**, but it fits extremely well in **Blue/Green Luffy**. I decided to play **Buggy** because I think there are some **matchups** where our small characters are not enough, and sometimes we really need to put bigger **6k bodies** on the **board**, especially against **decks** like **Blue/Yellow Nami**.

I do not think there is any real downside to this card, especially because we can always re-stand two **DON!!** with our **Leader effect**. When going first, developing **Buggy** on the **5 DON!! turn** is very strong, because he can bring out one of the **Prisoner** characters and draw one card.

He also basically generates a **Blocker** every turn, which can be extremely useful in **matchups** where both players are trying to race and pressure each other.

I think **Buggy** is a mandatory four-of, especially because we are playing many **Prisoner** cards. He is basically a **+1** while also cheating a lot of **DON!!**.

This card really reminds me of when we were playing **Jinbe** and **Wenvil**(?) in **Blue Doflamingo**. The concept is very similar, if not even better, because we are not putting real bricks in our **deck**. We are still drawing a card from him, while also developing **board**.

Another important reason to play **Buggy** is that, especially when going first, you can play **Buggy**, cheat out a **Prisoner**, draw one card, re-stand two **DON!!**, then play **You Can Be My Samurai** by resting both **Buggy** and the **Prisoner** character.

This line lets you draw two extra cards and generate a huge amount of value from a single turn.

Overall, I think **Buggy** is simply insane in this **deck**.

### Prisoner of Impel down

This card is very simple: **Prisoner of Impel Down** is just a **2k counter**.

The funny part is that we can play a lot of copies of this card. For now, I decided to play **12 copies** of it. I tried several different **2k counters** that we could include in this **deck**, but overall, at the moment, I feel comfortable with just playing this one.

Since the **deck** generates a lot of value, I like having my **hand** full of **2k counters**, especially to protect my **6k bodies**. In some **matchups**, I also want the consistency of seeing **Buggy** into **Prisoner** on curve.

There is not much to say about this card because it does not really have an effect, but we can always play it out off **Buggy**, and it can really protect us in the very late game.

It reminds me a lot of when we were playing **Red Rayleigh**. We would go extremely aggressive, our opponent would try to survive, and then we would simply have too many **counters** in **hand** for them to kill us.

I think **Prisoner of Impel Down** gives the **deck** a lot of consistency in terms of **counter power**. It prevents us from dying, buys us extra turns, and those extra turns often allow us to **aggro** very easily.

I think we could eventually go down to **10 copies**, but for now I feel comfortable with **12**.

### Boa Hancock

**Boa Hancock** is the strongest card in the **deck** and one of our main **win conditions** in a lot of **matchups**.

I think this card is simply insane. For **7 DON!!**, she is a **9k body**, which is already overstated. On top of that, she has **Unblockable**, a new keyword that can really help us close games, especially in the late game.

She becomes even stronger when we are able to set up a **board** for our **re-stand Events** and swing **lethal** with **Boa Hancock**. Most of the time, she will be our main **win condition** in games that go long, and she is also very difficult for most **decks** in the **meta** to remove.

Her **On Play** ability is also extremely powerful. We can freeze basically everything relevant in the current **meta**. Fortunately for us, there is no single **Luffy** character in the format that we cannot freeze, so the small downside of her effect is almost non-existent right now.

I am currently playing three copies of **Boa Hancock** because I do not feel like(?) we need to see her every game in order to win. Most of the time, we only need one copy to seal the game, delay our opponent's **board**, and create a situation where they cannot easily come back.

As I already mentioned in the **Mr.3** section, **Boa Hancock** combos extremely well with that card. In many games, we can also chain **Boa Hancock** into another **Boa Hancock**, locking the opponent's **board** two turns in a row while developing a huge body that is basically impossible to remove.

She also combos very well with **You Can Be My Samurai**, because she is a huge body and most **decks** in the **meta** cannot really deal with her. This means we can easily rest her for **You Can Be My Samurai** right after playing her and still feel safe.

Overall, **Boa Hancock** is just too good and represents one of the strongest reasons to play this **deck**.

### Gum-Gum Rain

**Gum-Gum Rain** is an option I decided to run mainly because I felt like sometimes I had too many bricks in **hand**, and I was also at **49 cards**.

We can search this card with our **searchers**, so it becomes a very easy way to unbrick our **hand** when needed. For now, I do not think **Gum-Gum Rain** is mandatory, but since I was missing only one slot, I think it was the best addition.

The card is very simple: it fixes our **hand**.

If we have multiple copies of cards like **Crocodile**, **Boa Hancock**, **You Can Be My Samurai**, or non-counter cards in general, **Gum-Gum Rain** helps us solve that problem very easily.

Since the **deck** does not have many direct ways to cycle away bad cards from **hand**, this card gives us a clean and simple way to fix awkward situations and improve our consistency.

### You can be my samurai!!

As I already mentioned several times, **You Can Be My Samurai** is one of the most interesting cards in this **deck**.

I am not fully convinced that this card will stay in the **decklist** forever, but for now I feel like it is simply too good in **Blue/Green Luffy**. It combos extremely well with many of the synergies I already explained in the previous card sections, and I still believe the card is very strong.

**You Can Be My Samurai** can generate a lot of value for us, and it is very easy to play because we almost always have those two extra **DON!!** available thanks to our **Leader effect**. In many **matchups**, we can simply rest our own **board** without any real downsides, especially when our characters have already generated value or are difficult for the opponent to remove.

For now, I am playing four copies. This gives me the consistency to see a large portion of the **deck** in most games. Sometimes it can feel like a brick in **hand**, but based on my testing so far, I think the card is too good to cut, especially at the beginning of the **meta**.

Since I do not want to build my **list** around any specific **matchup** yet, I decided to run four copies of **You Can Be My Samurai**. The card synergizes so well with the **deck** that I think it deserves the full playset for now.

Also, if anyone is wondering why I am playing an **OP01 card** that should have rotated away, this card was recently reprinted, so it is still legal in the current **format**.

### Let's go! To the navy headquarters!

As the last card of the **deck**, we have **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters**.

This is the card I already mentioned several times without actually naming it, and I think it is the main reason why I decided to play this **deck** in the first place.

A lot of people were initially reluctant about the power level of this card, but I think they simply did not play the **deck** enough to realize how insane it actually is. The amount of games I won because of this card, and the amount of extra swings I was able to generate with it, is just incredible.

I think that if you play the **deck** very well, **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters** becomes extremely broken.

On the other hand, I do not think you need to play four copies. The **deck** has several ways to find at least one copy, and in many games you can still win even without it. However, it is always important to have access to this option inside the **decklist**.

Right now, I am playing two copies. Since the **deck** draws a lot of cards and searches a lot, I usually see it whenever I need it. For now, I feel comfortable with two copies.

I think this card could eventually go up to three copies, but I would not go below two. In my opinion, **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters** is mandatory in the **deck**.

It is simply too good in many **matchups**, it works as one of our main **win conditions**, and it does not really have any downside since it can also be used as a **counter** defensively.

## 3.2 Tech cards

### Inazuma

When it comes to **Inazuma**, I think this card could be useful if we enter a **meta** where we actually need smaller **Blockers** in the **deck**.

Right now, I do not think it fits this **build**, because what I am trying to do is include mostly cards that generate value. For that reason, I would not include **Inazuma** in the current **list**.

That being said, I think it is a good **tech choice** if you want to run some very cheap **Blockers**.

### Jinbe

A lot of people are actually opting for **Jinbe** as a **2k counter**.

I tried this card quite a lot because I wanted to have more consistency with my **2-cost characters**, but I noticed that whenever I played **Jinbe**, I was basically giving myself a **minus one**. In the end, I decided not to run this card in my **decklist**.

I do not think the card itself is terrible, but I want to avoid drawing cards that are not useful for our main strategy. A lot of **2-cost characters** that we will cover later can be useful in the **endgame**, because you can develop them to activate the **re-stand Event**.

**Jinbe** can be a decent option because, in the worst-case scenario, he is still a **2k counter**. However, from my testing, I did not feel like this card was needed.

### Mr. 3 Galdino (2c)

The **2-cost Mr.3** is also a very good card that we could consider including in the **decklist**.

I see this card more as a **toolbox card** that we can include depending on the **meta**. It can really help us in the **endgame**, especially when we want to go for **lethal** with the **re-stand Event**, because it can act as one of the missing differently named characters on the **board**.

I think this card could be played as a two-of. If it had been a **2k counter**, it would have been broken, but since it does not have **2k counter**, I am not including it for the time being.

If we start seeing a lot of **Blockers** in the **meta**, this card might become a good inclusion. I do not think it is really needed in the early game, but it can be very useful in specific situations.

It can also be strong in the **mirror match**, but for now I do not feel like I want to play it. I will probably test two copies at some point, but from my testing so far, it was not that useful.

### Mr. 3 Galdino (2k counter)

When it comes to the **Mr.3 Galdino 2k counter**, I think this card could be played in the **deck**, because having different names is always very interesting for the deck's strategy.

Similar to **Jinbe**, this card does not really generate value by itself, but it gives us another option to enable **lethal** with **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters**, especially when we end up having four characters with different names on the **board**.

Having access to a **Mr.3 Galdino 2k counter** in **hand** is very nice, especially with boards built through **5-cost Buggy** plus **Prisoner of Impel Down**, or **Crocodile**. This card can enable bigger swings, because it basically recycles the **DON!!** we are using for the **re-stand Event**.

What is nice about this card is that we can play it, use its effect, give two **DON!!** to one of our **6k bodies**, and then assign the rest of our **DON!!** normally. Once we use **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters** to re-stand the whole **board**, **Mr.3 Galdino** will also re-stand, allowing us to use the effect again.

This means we can assign four more **DON!!** across two of our **6k characters**, turning them into stronger swings like **8k** and **10k**, which can be very relevant when setting up **lethal**.

Currently, I am trying this card as a **2-of** in my **decklist**. I know that in the current **decklist** we are playing **12 Prisoner of Impel Down**, but as you will also see in the last chapter, I am testing this card with very good results.

### Emporio Ivankov (3c)

When I first started testing the **deck**, I felt like the **3-cost Emporio Ivankov** was one of the best cards we could include, and a very good support card from previous sets.

However, the more I tested the card, the more I felt like it was not needed at all.

The low number of **2-cost characters** in the **deck** basically denies the first **On Play** effect, which almost never activates. On top of that, with the new **build** running **You Can Be My Samurai**, we usually have too many copies in **hand**, meaning that the second effect almost never comes up either.

Being a **4k body** is also not ideal, as we already mentioned when talking about the other **Ivankov**. For all these reasons, I do not think this card makes the cut.

Overall, I am not a big fan of **3-cost Emporio Ivankov**, and I do not think it will be played in the future.

### Mr. 2 Bon kurei (4c)

**4-cost Mr.2 Bon Clay** was a card I was very excited about when the **deck** was first revealed.

However, after realizing the direction we wanted to take with the **deck**, I eventually decided not to include this card. I tested it a little bit, but it felt too clunky and too expensive for the current **build**.

What I realized while testing **Blue/Green Luffy** is that, most of the time, you do not really need to rest your opponent's **characters**, except maybe in the **mirror match**.

For that reason, I do not think this card is needed right now.

It is also not very good defensively, so it ends up feeling like a glass cannon that costs **4 DON!!**, which is simply too expensive for what it does.

### Monkey D. Luffy (4c)

**4-cost Luffy** follows basically the same principle as **4-cost Mr.2 Bon Clay**.

I think this card is too expensive for the **deck**. I tested it a little bit at the beginning, but every time I had it, it felt too costly and did not really accomplish what we wanted to do with the **deck**.

This could be a good card in a very specific scenario where we actually need to play a **Blocker**, but it is very hard to protect and it is also expensive to add another **4-cost character** to the **deck**.

Currently, I prefer keeping the **deck** very cheap in terms of **DON!! curve**, except for the **7-cost slot**, which is reserved for **Boa Hancock**, which is a real powerhouse.

This card also did not make the cut because it has no **counter**. As we mentioned before, it is not always easy for us to cycle bad cards out of our **hand**, so I want to minimize the number of bricks in my **decklist** as much as possible.

### Miss Olive

**Miss Olive** is basically a worse version of **4-cost Crocodile**.

She has **counter**, and yes, she has the ability to cycle your **hand**, but she is too expensive for the body she provides. If she had been a **7k body**, I would have considered this card, but for now I do not think she is worth it.

The **5-cost slot** is currently taken by **Buggy**, and I do not think there is a better play than **Buggy** in that slot.

For the time being, I think **Miss Olive** is not good enough and does not fit the **deck** very well.

### Crocodile (6c)

**6-cost Crocodile** is simply a very slow card.

Honestly, I do not think this card is intended for this **deck**. It is too expensive, and it does not really give us anything better than what **7-cost Boa Hancock** already provides.

The biggest issue is the limitation on his effect, since he can only freeze a rested **character**. This makes the card much worse, especially compared to the other options we have access to.

On top of that, his body is too weak for the amount of **DON!!** he costs.

For these reasons, I do not think **6-cost Crocodile** fits this **deck**, and overall I do not think the card is good enough.

### Brook

**Brook** is one of the most interesting options we could include in the **deck**.

I think this card should basically be seen as an **Event card** that can rest something and re-stand one of our **characters**. I was playing **Brook** in the **decklist** at the beginning, but eventually I decided to cut it.

Even though **Brook** is not an **Impel Down card**, we can still play it, sacrifice it, and use our **Leader effect**, so the card itself is not bad.

I just feel like **Brook** was too situational for the playstyle I wanted to follow, and it was also a bit too expensive for me.

That being said, I still think this card is playable. We might see it in some strong **lists** in the future. If you want to include **Brook**, I would recommend playing it as a two-of, especially to combo with **Boa Hancock**.

### I Know You're Strong... So I'll Go All Out from the Very Start!!!

**I Know You're Strong... So I'll Go All Out from the Very Start!!!** has been one of the most staple cards in the **green** color over the last sets.

I think this card is really good. It also does not have any downside, same as **Let's Go to the Navy Headquarters**, because we can always use it as a **+3000 Counter** defensively.

For the time being, however, I am not sure if I want to play this card, because I still need to see how the **meta** is going to settle. I think this card can help a lot in the **mirror match** and against **PY Roronoa**.

That being said, in general I felt like I rarely had two good targets to freeze with this card, because I was already combining this type of effect with **Mr.3** and **Boa Hancock** most of the time.

For now, I am not playing this card, but I still believe it is a good option that we might eventually see in many **lists**. I would probably play between two and three copies if the **meta** becomes more favorable for it.

I also think this card is better than **Electrical Luna** in this **deck**.

Overall, I like the card, but for now I am not including it because I want to test the waters before the **meta** fully settles.

### DMG (Dead man's game)

**DMG (Dead Man's Game)** is an interesting option that we could fit into the **deck**.

I think this could be a strong one-of option if we start seeing many **decks** trying to play as defensively as possible against us. From my testing, there are several situations in the current **meta** where denying either two **Blockers**, or one **Blocker** plus one open **DON!!**, can be very relevant.

This is very similar to what **Green Mihawk** was trying to do with this card, using it for a cheaper cost and creating strong finishing turns. In this **deck**, because of the **Leader effect**, we can effectively play it and make our **lethal** turns easier, especially against **decks** that rely on **Blockers** or defensive **Events**.

The usage of this card is very similar to **6-cost Brook**. Both cards can help us shut down defensive setups and make our **lethal** turns easier, especially against **decks** that rely on **Blockers** or defensive **Events**.

Because of that, I think **DMG (Dead Man's Game)** is a card that might see play as a one-of in the future, depending on how defensive the **meta** becomes.

### Gum gum champion rifle

**Gum-Gum Champion Rifle** was really hyped at the beginning of this **set**'s release, but I do not believe this card is very good.

What I am seeing in my **deck** is that almost every **deck** has access to very small **characters** that can simply sit on the **board** to deny this card. Because of that, **Gum-Gum Champion Rifle** feels too situational, and the payoff is too random for it to be consistently useful.

Also, keeping one **DON!!** open in this **deck** is very rare if you want to play it at its maximum potential.

For these reasons, I think this card is just bad, and I do not think it is going to make the cut.

### Hound blaze

I have seen a lot of **lists** from Asia running **Hound Blaze**.

I think this card is really good in the **mirror match**, but I do not feel like it is useful in many other **matchups**. Following the same principle of not wanting too many bricks or situational cards in the **deck**, I decided not to run it.

I think **Hound Blaze** would definitely see play if we started seeing a lot of **mirror matches**, because in that scenario the card becomes very strong. However, outside of the **mirror**, I do not think there are enough relevant targets in the **meta** to justify playing it.

Maybe it can be useful against **BY Teach**, but that is already a very good **matchup** for us, so I would not build my **decklist** around improving it.

### Electrical luna

**Electrical Luna** is also one of the most staple cards in **green**.

As I mentioned before, I do not think this card is going to be better than **I Know You're Strong... So I'll Go All Out from the Very Start!!!** in this **deck**. The reason is that it is very unlikely for our opponent to have enough rested **characters** on the **board** to justify **Electrical Luna** over that card.

**I Know You're Strong... So I'll Go All Out from the Very Start!!!** freezes two **characters**, while **Electrical Luna** can freeze the whole **board**. However, most of the time, our opponent will not have more than two relevant rested **characters** anyway.

For this reason, I still believe **I Know You're Strong... So I'll Go All Out from the Very Start!!!** is better in this **deck**.

Right now, I do not think I am going to play **Electrical Luna**. If we eventually decide to play more **Events** that freeze the **board**, then we might include this card as a two-of.

## Conclusion

In general, I do not think this **deck** will find its final **decklist** in the first weeks of play.

Compared to many of the **decks** I covered in my previous guides, I believe **Blue/Green Luffy** has access to a very wide variety of **tech cards** that we can play. Because of that, I think the final **decklist** develops throughout the **meta**.

I still believe that, at the beginning of the **meta**, the best **list** is the most generic one. Then, as the **format** develops and we understand which **decks** are going to be the most popular, we will be able to fine-tune the **decklist** for those specific **matchups**.

For the time being, I recommend trying every **tech card** that you consider worth playing. I tested all of them, and I still believe some of them might eventually see play in my **list** in the future.

As usual, I will try to keep this guide updated with the latest **decklist** as we move forward into the **meta**.
