## Vs Red Blue Lucy (OP16-002)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Lucy" (Dressrosa/Revolutionary Army), 5000 power, OP15-002, 4 LIFE, red/blue. Text: "When Attacking / On Your Opponent's Attack: You may trash any number of Event or Stage cards from your hand. This Leader gains +1000 power during this battle for every card trashed. Activate:Main / Once Per Turn: If you have activated an Event card with a base cost of 3 or more during this turn, draw 1 card."]

**Favorability:** Favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

Going second disrupts their preferred curve of chaining consecutive **4C Cavendish** or **4C Bartolomeo** into **7C Sabo**. It also denies them a draw on their first turn, making it easier to pressure them out of resources early.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**

**The Macro Gameplan:**

Execute your standard wide strategy by deploying two or more characters per turn. Attack frequently at a 6k threshold or higher, as **Red Blue Lucy** can struggle to defend against continuous 6k pressure. In the late game, use freeze events to lock down their board, while accounting for a potential **10C Roger** play to avoid losing your advantage.

**General Tips**

**Curve and Board Presence**

- **Curve Sequence:** Play your 1-cost and 2-cost characters in the early game to build a wide board. Transition into the **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down** package in the mid-game, followed by **7C Boa** to maintain pressure.
- **Weenie Advantage:** If the opponent fails to develop small characters early, your rested small characters can safely absorb their leader's attacks.
- **Overwhelming Removal:** While Lucy has reliable removal plays, they typically can only remove one character per turn. Building a very wide board ensures they cannot keep pace with your threats.

**Pressuring Resources**

- **Spamming 6k Attacks:** Target the leader with consecutive 6k attacks. If the opponent lacks their Stage card, they will struggle to protect themselves because their deck naturally runs out of 2k counters quickly. **1C Viola** is one of their primary 2k counters, but they often prefer to play her on the field as a searcher and a small character rather than keeping her in hand for defense.

**Board Control and Freezing**

- **Stalling High-Cost Threats:** Use **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to freeze their **7C Sabo** and **4C Cavendish** characters while you continue attacking with your wide board.
- **Punishing Blocks:** If the opponent uses **7C Sabo** to block your attacks, you can use freeze events like **3C Electrical Luna** and **1C All Out** to keep him rested and frozen on their following turn.

**Defensive Awareness**

- **Managing Double Attack and Roger:** The opponent's primary win condition involves aggressive Double Attack leader swings via **7C Flame Flame Fruit**, followed by a **10C Roger** play for a surprise lethal push. Ensure that you leave up open DON!! to prevent a Double Attack from connecting.
- **Playing Around 10C Roger:** If you reduce the opponent to 0 life early in the game, always calculate your defenses against a potential **10C Roger** turn and play defensively to prevent a sudden loss. If needed, you can keep them at 1 life and reduce their hand cards first by swinging at their board.

**Tech Cards**

- **3C Electrical Luna / 1C All Out:** Used to freeze their board, keeping their rested threats locked down.

## Vs Red Blue Ace (OP13-002)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Portgas.D.Ace" (Whitebeard Pirates), 6000 power, OP13-002, 3 LIFE, red/blue. Text: "On Your Opponent's Attack / Once Per Turn: You may trash 1 card from your hand: Give up to 1 of your opponent's Leader or Characters -2000 power during this battle. DON!! x1 / Once Per Turn: When you receive damage or your Character with 6000 base power or more is K.O'd, draw 1 card."]

**Favorability:** Slightly Favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

Going second denies their preferred on-curve sequence of playing **8C I am Whitebeard** followed by **10C Newgate**.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**, or the **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down** package.

**Macro Gameplan:**

Go wide from turn one. Red Blue Ace runs a 6k power base, but this is not a problem since all of your characters can attack for 6k without issue. Aggro them down through the mid-game, push them to 0 life as fast as possible, then close with the **7C Boa + 6C Brook** finish.

**General Tips**

**Early Game Curve**

- **6k Pressure:** 6k power leaders are fine opponents since most of your characters attack for 6k anyway. If you can get going with an early aggro hand and spam 6k attacks, you will whittle down their hand quickly.
- **Curve Sequence:** Curve out with 2-cost characters in the early game, into **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down**, into **7C Boa**.

**Key Card Interactions**

- **2C Bon Clay Usage:** Consider saving his attack or bouncing and replaying him with **4C Crocodile**, since he can still attack the leader efficiently even if your opponent buffs their leader through **7C Zoro** or **8C I am Whitebeard**.
- **Alternate Win Conditions:** Do not tunnel vision on **7C Boa** alone. Red Blue Ace now runs **6C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to freeze opponent's cards. If **7C Boa** is being disabled, pivot to **2C Galdino** + 6k attackers + the board restand event, or set up **2C Bon Clay** attacking twice with **6C Brook** as a win condition.

**Capitalizing on DON!! Gaps**

- **Leader Ability Windows:** Take advantage of turns where they do not have DON!! attached to their leader, since they will be forced to counter out of multiple attacks because they will miss out on their leader ability draw if they don't counter out.

**Tech Cards**

- **7C Gravity Blade:** Bottom-decks their **4C Marco** and **6C Boa**.
- **4C Rush Luffy:** Rests their **10C Newgate** in the late game.

## Vs Red Shanks (OP09-001)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Shanks" (The Four Emperors/Red Hair Pirates), 5000 power, OP09-001, 5 LIFE, red. Text: "Once Per Turn: You may activate this effect when your opponent attacks. Give up to 1 of your opponent's leader or characters Power -1000 for the turn."]

**Favorability:** Slightly Favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

You want to go second to delay their 10 DON!! turn to delay their power play of 5C Beckman + 10C Shanks.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**, or the combo of **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down**.

Macro Gameplan:

Follow the normal gameplan of going wide and attacking for multiple 6k attacks with your wide board. Red Shanks runs a lot of bricks and they tend to quickly run out of counters when dealing with an aggro deck that goes wide quickly. If you go second, you can often put them to 0 life before they even reach the 10 DON!! turn. From there, **7C Boa** or any other character can finish them off before they can stabilize with their boss cards.

**General Tips**

**Attack Sequencing**

- **Hitting for 6k:** Hit them for 6k attacks repeatedly so that they can't get a free counter with their leader ability. Once they have already used their leader ability, you can consider swinging with 5k attacks.
- **Playing on Curve:** Just try to play on curve and keep swinging 6k attacks. Try to notice how your opponent is countering. It's quite easy to get a read on them once they've run out of counters, and you can capitalize on that.

**Reading the Counter Supply**

- **Recognizing the Window:** The usual way to win this matchup is to tax their hand repeatedly and bring them down to 0 life before they even reach 10 DON!!, then use **6C Brook** to attack twice, ideally with **7C Boa**. At that point they should be out of counters, and usually any character will do.

**Handling Their Defensive Tools**

- **4C Lucky Roo:** The main way they can slow down your aggression. Use **4C Rush Luffy** to rest it and prevent it from blocking, or bottom-deck it with **7C Gravity Blade**.

**Tech Cards**

- **4C Rush Luffy:** Rests **4C Lucky Roo** to prevent it from blocking.
- **7C Gravity Blade:** Bottom-decks **4C Lucky Roo** and other early-mid game characters.

## Vs Black Yamato (OP16-079)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Yamato" (Land of Wano), 5000 power, OP16-079, 5 LIFE, black, watermarked "SAMPLE". Text: "When a [Land of Wano] type Character card is played from your trash, that Character gains Rush during this turn. (This card can attack on the turn in which it is played.)"]

**Favorability:** Slightly Unfavored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

You want to go second to prevent their on-curve **4C Kinemon** into **6C Yamato**.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**.

**Macro Gameplan**

This is a race. You cannot beat them in a long game due to them having more attacks than you in the late game. Go wide and aggro them down early. In the mid-game, stun their **8C Yamato** copies with **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** and try to set up your restand event to win. You are usually 1 turn behind them in terms of setting up lethal, so your ultimate goal is to stall that 1 turn just enough to secure lethal.

**General Tips**

**Defensive Approach**

- **Counter Priority:** Black Yamato is an explosive deck that has a lot of attacks from the mid-game onwards. They will always have big 8k attacks in the mid to late game, so as much as possible you want to counter out of attacks that only require 1 card to counter, since they will for sure have bigger attacks later on.

**Taking Free Blocks**

- **Assessing Free Block Value:** They will usually start their turns with 5k attacks from their **Yamato** leader and characters, which will give you the opportunity to get free blocks with your 6k power blockers. However, be wary because a good Yamato player will use their 8k **Yamato** to attack into your blocker right after, likely even using **2C Otama** to reduce its power then attack into it to ensure the kill. Before taking the free block, assess if you need it for your attack turn, or if you're fine using it as a sacrificial character to absorb attacks. This will depend on how much counter you have in hand and how many attacks your opponent has left. Always count the number of attacks remaining that your opponent has before you take free blocks.

**Winning Conditions**

- **Drawing Dependency:** Whether you win or lose mostly depends on how Black Yamato draws. If they draw well and play correctly, they should win most of the time, mainly because you are usually always 1 turn behind them in terms of getting lethal. However, Black Yamato has consistency issues and they have a lot of games where they whiff their hand, so you end up winning a good amount of games against them as well. There is also a factor of experience difference in the matchup.
- **No Early Weenies:** If they don't have any weenies early, all your early game characters function as pseudo-blockers since they will absorb attacks that would have gone to your leader.
- **Missing Curve Pieces:** If they miss out on their **4C Kinemon** or **6C Yamato** on curve, you have time to set up your own lethal.
- **Matchup Inexperience:** If they aren't familiar with the matchup and focus too much on attacking life and ignore your rested 6k bodies.

**Winning Line**

- **Lethal Setup:** Your main way to win is to poke them down while making sure that you set up blockers and counters just enough to survive the lethal turn, then lethal them back with a board restand event or a **6C Brook** restand play. Most of the time, you have to make bets when going for lethal: either hope that they don't have the counters in hand, or when defending their attacks, accept that your life cards may have counters, because you need to save your combo pieces in hand: **6C Brook**, board restand event.

## Vs Purple Sengoku (OP16-060)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Sengoku" (Navy), 5000 power, OP16-060, 5 LIFE, purple, watermarked "SAMPLE". Text: "Activate:Main: You may return 8 of your active DON!! cards to your DON!! deck: Play up to 3 [Admiral] type Character cards with different card names from your hand."]

**Favorability:** Favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

Preferred, but turn order does not matter much in this matchup.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**.

**Macro Gameplan**

Go wide and go aggro. Purple Sengoku cannot really survive your early onslaught, and even their power play of playing three Admirals from hand does not do much to stop your aggression.

**General Tips**

**Early Game Dominance**

- **Exploiting Their Ramp Dependency:** Purple Sengoku is very weak in the early to mid-game, as most of their cards are focused on ramping quickly to their 7 DON!! threshold. They have negligible tools to stop your aggression. Follow the normal go-wide aggro gameplan and attack for a wide board of 5k and 6k attackers.
- **Forcing Attacks into Weenies:** By the time they play their 7-cost Admirals, you will still be at a healthy life total because their attacks are forced into your weenies, while you have likely already depleted their hand and put them at low life.

**Late Game Lethals**

- **Multiple Closing Lines:** Once they are low on life, you can often lethal them however you like. You can set up a lethal with the restand event, put them down to low life then use double attacks with **6C Brook**, or whichever lethal is available in hand.

**Key Threats to Track**

- **7C Kuzan:** Can disable blockers, so keep this in mind if your opponent might be going for a desperation lethal.
- **7C Borsalino:** Can turn himself into a blocker, but this is usually no issue since you can simply use **7C Boa** on him to stop him from resting, and a single blocker won't be enough to stop your board restand event in the late game.

## Vs Red Ace (OP16-001)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Portgas.D.Ace" (Whitebeard Pirates), 5000 power, OP16-001, 5 LIFE, red, watermarked "SAMPLE". Text: "Activate:Main / Once Per Turn: Up to 1 of your [Monkey.D.Luffy] Characters or up to 1 of your Characters with a type including "Whitebeard Pirates" with 8000 power or more, gains Rush during this turn."]

**Favorability:** Even

**Turn Order:** Go Second

We want to go second in this matchup because we want to take away their curve of playing **1C Stage** into **6C Vista** to clear your weenies. By going second, you get a turn where your weenies can attack without being immediately cleared by **6C Vista**.

**Mulligan Priority:** 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones, 5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down

**Macro Gameplan**

In the early game, you want to set up weenies and attack aggressively with them as soon as possible, in order to rely on the chance they they will absorb attacks before the opponent has opportunity to get rid of them through 6C Vista. Go wide with 6k power or higher chjaracters and keep hitting the opposing leader, while you freeze their attackers with 7C Boa and 4C Galdino. Be wary of their 8k rusher characters, always keep this in mind when defending a possible lethal or when taking free blocks.

**General Tips**

**Playing Around 6C Vista**

- **Going Second Advantage:** If you go second, you will have a full turn where you can attack with your weenies and your opponent does not yet have enough DON!! to play **6C Vista**. This means these weenies will likely have absorbed attacks already before your opponent's **Vista** turn.
- **Going First:** Your opponent needs to have **6C Vista** on 6 DON!!, as well as play a **Moby Dick Stage** in the early turns in order to attach a DON!! to **Vista** and enable his On Attack effect. If they miss out on the early Stage, they can play **Vista** but **Vista** will not be able to clear your weenies.
- **Vista Already Cleared Your Board:** In their best case scenario that they clear your weenies on curve, it's not the end of the world because these weenies have already replaced themselves with a draw on play and they have likely already been able to attack. For the following turns, you can use **7C Boa** or **4C Galdino** to freeze **6C Vista** and stop him from clearing further weenies.
- **Keep Playing Weenies:** Do not hesitate on playing weenies because "**Vista** will just clear them." Still play them out since they replace themselves with a draw on play and they cycle your hand.

**Managing 4C Luffy**

- **Hitting Through the Power Boost:** **4C Luffy** is another card that makes them 7k power, but they still have to discard cards and if you have a wide board you can still tax them.
- **2C Bon Clay:** Can copy the opponent's leader's power to still hit for 8k or higher even if they boost their power.
- **7C Boa + 6C Brook:** Double Attack combos are still difficult for them to defend.

**Managing Their Win Conditions**

- **8k Rush Characters:** Red Ace's power comes from their ability to have 8k rushers that they play from hand every turn from the mid-game onwards. Be careful when taking free blocks on smaller attacks, as an 8k attack may come to swing on your rested blocker. Be careful when taking life cards from small attacks, since you might go down too low when your opponent attacks you with an 8k rusher. Try to counter out of attacks that only require 1 card to counter as best you can.
- **8C Whitebeard:** The most threatening character they can put out, mainly because he grants their leader a 2k power boost and Double Attack on their attack turn. A common way to lose this matchup is if you're unprepared for a high power double attack and you go down to zero unexpectedly in the late game. Once your opponent reaches the 8 DON!! turn, prepare blockers and counter events for defending against their possible Double Attack.

**Tech Cards**

- **7C Gravity Blade:** A very strong card in this matchup, especially if you play it on curve and bottom-deck their **4C Luffy** and **6C Vista**.

## Vs Green Mihawk (OP14-020)

> [IMMAGINE: Leader card "Dracule Mihawk" (The Seven Warlords of the Sea), 5000 power, OP14-020, 5 LIFE, green. Text: "If your opponent's Leader has the <Slash> attribute, this Leader gains +1000 power. Activate:Main / Once Per Turn: You may rest 1 of your cards: If there is a Character with a cost of 5 or more, set up to 3 of your DON!! cards as active. Then, you cannot play Character cards from your hand during this turn."]

**Favorability:** Slightly favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

You want to go second to follow your own curve, but also to delay their **6C Law** and **10C Law and Bepo** turns.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**.

**Macro Gameplan**

Follow the go-wide aggro gameplan in the early to mid-game, poking them for wide 6k attacks. Be careful of **1C All Out** and **3C Electrical Luna**, as this is their most common way to win this matchup. Do not over-defend your rested characters since they might just freeze them with freeze spells. Use **7C Boa** to disable their most impactful attackers, then set up a board restand event turn in the late game.

**General Tips**

**Early Game Approach**

- **Weenie Deployment:** Green Mihawk needs to draw into their anti-weenie tools to contest your board early. Play weenies early and attack with them as soon as possible.
- **Attack Redirection:** If your opponent has early weenies to contest the board, most of your attacks are often better off redirected to the leader, since Mihawk can usually fill out the board with more weenies even if you clear them. It's better if your attacks always either ask for a hand card or take a life card, rather than being wasted to clear the board.
- **Counter Depletion:** Green Mihawk has very few 2k counters. You will likely find them countering out with counter events once they've run out of 2k counters to stop your 6k attacks.

**Handling Freeze Events**

- **Do Not Over-Defend:** Freeze events are their main tools to stop your aggression and survive into the late game. If they are hitting your rested characters, do not over-defend them unless your opponent no longer has DON!! available for their freeze events. If you defend them with counter cards, your opponent can just freeze your characters, making you waste cards for nothing since they can usually keep these characters rested for consecutive turns.
- **Minimize Freeze Value:** Consider skipping some attacks altogether in order to give your opponent minimum value for their freeze events, for example just leaving them 1 rested character to freeze instead of two, to make their freeze events inefficient.

**Mid-game Control**

- **Boa and Galdino Targets:** Use **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to stop their most impactful characters from attacking, commonly **5C Smoker** in the mid-game and **9C Mihawk** in the late game.

**The 10 DON!! Turn**

- **Preventing the 7k Leader:** Their 10 DON!! turn is very likely a **10C Law and Bepo** play. Before your opponent reaches 10 DON!!, leave your whole board active and skip all your character attacks altogether. The main reason is to prevent them from getting the 7k leader power on the following turn.
- **Closing the Game:** In the late game, they are likely already low on life and low on counters. If you prevent them from becoming a 7k leader, they will likely just die to your wide board of attacks on the following turn.
