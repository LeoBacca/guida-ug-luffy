## Vs Purple Enel (OP15-058)

**Favorability:** Slightly Unfavored

**Turn Order:** Go First

Going first steals the opponent's ideal curve and delays their first 6C Enel.

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones,** or a combo of **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down.**

**Macro Gameplan**

This matchup is a battle of resource attrition. Your goal is to develop two characters per turn, creating a wide board that your opponent is forced to answer while you chip away at their life with constant 6k attacks. Establish as many 6k power bodies as possible to force the Enel player to spend combo cards from their hand. Control their offense using freeze cards, and pivot to a lethal push once **Purple Enel**'s resources are depleted.

**General Tips**

This matchup comes down to three core pillars: the **Resource Battle**, the **Weenie Battle**, and the **Fortress/Lethal Battle**.

**The Resource Battle**

- **Go Wide: Purple Enel** relies on a control strategy to clear your board. Counter this by deploying two or more characters per turn and keeping 6k power bodies on the field to force out their removal events.
- **Drain Their Counters:** Active 6k bodies require at least two combo cards for **Enel** to clear, usually a combination of **OC Kiten** and **OC Mamaragan**. Forcing them to spend these events on your board removes the 2k counters they need to defend their life later.
- **Force Mistakes:** Continuous 6k attacks at their life force a difficult choice: defend their life or burn a combo event. Consistently forcing this choice can lead to micro-mistakes that accumulate over the course of the game.
- **Track the Trash:** Check your opponent's trash regularly, especially in the late game. Counting their used events helps you determine two things:
  - If it is safe to go for lethal because their counter events are exhausted.
  - If you can safely defend behind your blockers because they lack the events to bypass or clear them.

**The Weenie Battle**

Both **Blue-Green Luffy** and **Purple Enel** use small, low-cost characters ("weenies") that replace themselves by drawing a card on play. Both leaders can also attach DON!! to turn these small bodies into active threats. Because of this, both players will constantly contest each other's small characters.

As the **Blue-Green Luffy** player, deciding whether to attack their weenies or their leader is critical. Every attack directed away from their leader gives them fewer tough choices regarding whether to counter out or save a combo piece.

- **The Golden Rule for Weenies:** If attacking their small characters results in the opponent having fewer attacks on the following turn (meaning you can completely clear them), clear them. If attacking their weenies does not change the number of attacks they can throw at you, ignore them and attack the leader.
- **Hide Your Weenies:** You do not need to swing with your own small characters immediately, especially if the opponent can easily clear them on the following turn. Keep them active and build your board to force the opponent to use cards like **OC El Thor** to get rid of them. It is usually best to attack with multiple characters in a single turn to force the opponent to choose a target, instead of presenting a single clear target each turn.
- **The Lowroll Exception:** If **Enel** fails to develop small characters in the early game, change your plan. Aggressively attack with your own weenies to bait leader attacks into them, effectively turning your small characters into pseudo-blockers.

**The Fortress/Lethal Battle**

Your turn-by-turn goal is to reduce your opponent's resources by poking their leader while ensuring your own defenses are strong enough to survive their turn.

- **Do Not Force Lethal:** Avoid pushing for game unless it is an absolute emergency (e.g., you will lose next turn if you don't). If lethal isn't guaranteed, poke with attacks to drain their hand, defend yourself, and establish a defensive setup.
- **Your Defensive Toolkit:**
  - **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to freeze and stop their **6C Enels** from attacking.
  - **4C Crocodile** and **6C Prisoner of Impel Down** as blockers.
  - **1C Restand Event** or any other 3k counter events.
  - Freeze tech events like **1C All Out** and **3C Electrical Luna**.
- **Patience is Key:** If lethal isn't open, set up your blockers, freeze their attackers, and leave active DON!! up to threaten your defensive counter events. Be patient, especially if **Enel's** hand size is still healthy. **Purple Enel** usually only has 1–2 quality attacks per turn if you are actively contesting their board.
- **The Lethal Push:** When the time comes to go for game, attack for 7k power or higher. Use **6C Brook** and **4C Rush Luffy** to bypass blockers and attack multiple times. In rare scenarios, you can also use your **1C Board Restand** Event if the opponent leaves themselves exposed.

**Key Matchup Notes**

- **Clear Rested 5k Bodies:** If the opponent leaves a 5k body rested on the board (such as **4C Franky**), attack and clear it unless you have an guaranteed lethal line.
- **The Holly Problem:** An early **3C Holly** is a major threat. If you take an early double-attack from Holly, your chances of winning drop significantly. Always save active DON!! for counter events to prevent Holly's attack from connecting. Once **Holly** is rested, prioritize attacking and clearing it.
- **Navigating 0C Lightning Dragon:** This is the card they use to keep your rested characters frozen for consecutive turns. They get maximum value by targeting your rested 6k blockers, **4C Crocodile** and **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**. **Enel** will often poke you with a 5k attack to bait these characters into blocking just so they can freeze them with **0C Lightning Dragon**.
  - When deciding whether to take the free block, ask yourself how punishing it would be if that blocker gets frozen. It is most punishing if you have very few characters on board and desperately need that 6k body as an attacker on your next turn. In those situations, it is often better to counter out of the 5k attack from hand to preserve your attacker, especially if you are setting up a lethal push for next turn. (Note: **Enel** will often be forced to use **0C Lightning Dragon** anyway just to DON!! minus and cycle their hand).
  - **Take the free block** when you don't mind the character being frozen because you already have plenty of other attackers, or when your opponent already has multiple copies of **0C Lightning Dragon** in the trash, meaning the probability of them holding more is low.
- **Respecting 4C Law:** 4C Law is a significant swing card if they successfully trigger its On Play effect to force you to discard 2 cards. Because this matchup is a resource war, you must avoid hitting the 7-card hand threshold that activates him. If you already have a strong hand, proactively counter out of early attacks to keep your hand size lean and safe from **4C Law**. Conversely, if your hand is weak and you need to draw, take the life damage and accept the risk of **4C Law**.
- **Stay Focused:** No matter how difficult the board state looks, keep building your board and chipping away at them. This is a pure war of attrition. If you can maintain a decent draw engine through the early-to-mid game, **Purple Enel** will eventually run out of removal and events in the late game, opening up your window to win.
- Tech Card Considerations: **3C Jet Culverin:** Excellent for cleanly removing their 4-cost characters. **1C All Out / 3C Electrical Luna:** Great for freezing their board, though keep in mind **Purple Enel** can occasionally play around this using **0C Varie.**

## Vs Blue Yellow Nami (OP11-041)

> [IMMAGINE: Cheat sheet titled "OP16 CHEAT SHEET - OP16 GUFFY VS OP11 NAMI" by RAPHTERRA. Left side shows the Nami leader card (5000 power, Straw Hat Crew, OP11-041). USE 6C BROOK TO ATTACK TWICE WITH 7C BOA AS YOUR MAIN LATE GAME FINISHER ONCE NAMI IS AT LOW LIFE. AVOID RUSHING, POKE TO DRAIN THEIR RESOURCES, AND TURTLE BEHIND YOUR BLOCKERS AND COUNTERS OR FREEZE CARDS UNTIL LETHAL IS GUARANTEED." **Favorability:** Favored

**Turn Order:** Go First

Going first lets you take the aggressive initiative, making it difficult for **Nami** to safely activate her leader ability. It also denies them their preferred curve of **5C Nami** into **7C Robin.**

**Mulligan Priority: 2C Bon Clay, 4C Crocodile,** or a combo of **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down.**

**Macro Gameplan:**

You are the aggressor in this matchup. Your gameplan centers on forcing your opponent to use resources from their hand. Aim to attack for 7k power every turn to force them to either discard two cards or take life damage. While **Blue-Yellow Nami** can buff her leader to 7k power, this is manageable because your deck runs characters that can attack into that statline, such as **2C Bon Clay, 7C Boa,** and your 6k power characters.

**General Tips**

**Maintaining 7k Pressure**

- **Early Game Discards:** Hit their leader for 7k power at least once every turn from the first turn you can attack. In the early game, **Nami** players prefer not to take life damage to avoid going over their leader ability's 8-card hand threshold. They will often use two cards to block, reducing their hand size.
- **The Bon Clay Engine: 2C Bon Clay** copies the opponent's leader's power, meaning even if **Nami** buffs herself, **2C Bon Clay** can hit her leader for 8k or higher. You can reserve **2C Bon Clay** for the later stages of the game, attach 2 DON!!, and attack for 9k. If you have **4C Crocodile** in hand, you can attack with **2C Bon Clay** early, then bounce and replay him with **4C Crocodile** to attack again on the following turn.
- **Using 6k Characters:** You can also attack **Nami**'s 7k statline by attaching DON!! to your 6k power characters, such as **4C Crocodile, 5C Buggy,** and **5C Prisoner of Impel Down** (or **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**). You can use the 2k counter **2C Galdino** to efficiently attach DON!! to these 6k characters.

**Capitalizing on Unattached Turns**

**Blue-Yellow Nami** requires a DON!! attached to her leader to reach 7k power. Take advantage of turns where the opponent leaves no DON!! attached to their leader. On these turns, you do not need to attack for 7k; instead, treat her like a 5k leader and attack wide with 5k and 6k swings.

- **Common Openings:** This typically happens on their 4 DON!! turn if they play **4C Marco** or **4C Kiku,** or on their 7 DON!! turn if they play **7C Robin.**

**Execution and Positioning**

- **The 7C Boa Win Condition: 7C Boa** is a 9k unblockable character that can reliably attack **Nami** without any DON!! attachments. Once **Nami** is low on cards, attacking twice with **7C Boa** via **6C Brook**'s restand effect is a reliable way to close out the game. Do not over-rely on this combo, however, as the opponent can play **4C Galdino** to freeze your **7C Boa** for your attack turn.
- **The Weenie Bait:** You can use **2C Daz Bones** or **1C Luffy (Searcher)** to test your opponent's matchup knowledge. These small characters are normally inefficient to attack with because you must attach at least 2 DON!! just to reach the 7k threshold. If you have a choice between attaching 2 DON!! to your leader or a small character to attack for 7k, choose the character. This presents a choice for the **Nami** player; if they choose to attack into the rested **2C Daz Bones** on their turn, it functions as a pseudo-blocker by absorbing an attack meant for your leader. A knowledgeable **Nami** player will ignore it, but unprepared opponents may take the bait.

**Calculated Patience**

Be patient and do not rush for lethal if it is not secure. While you are the aggressor, your deck has the tools to sit behind blockers, freeze cards, and counter events if needed. As a Yellow deck, **Nami** has access to life triggers that can stop your lethal push, alongside healing tools like **5C Borsalino** that can delay your win. If lethal is not guaranteed, keep poking to drain their hand and establish your defenses instead of overextending.

**Tech Card Considerations**

- **2C Galdino** (2k Counter): Helps efficiently boost your 6k power characters to the 7k threshold.
- **6C Brook:** Used to enable multiple attacks with your high-power characters.
- **7C Gravity Blade:** Used to bottom-deck opposing characters with *On KO* effects, such as **4C Marco, 4C Kiku,** and **5C Nami.**

**A Note on Matchup Consensus:** The general consensus and statistics often label this as an unfavored matchup for **Blue-Green Luffy**. However, high-level **Blue-Green Luffy** players generally consider the matchup to be favored for us. Follow the gameplan, test the matchup, and focus on the resource engine rather than public tier lists.

## Vs Purple Yellow Rosinante (OP12-061)

Right side sections: MATCH UP KEY CARDS, MULLIGAN PRIORITY, OPPONENT'S KEY CARDS, TECH CARDS. Chibi Luffy artwork bottom right.]

**Favorability:** Slightly Favored

**Turn Order:** Go First

Allows you to take the aggressive initiative and prevents their preferred sequence of playing **4C Law** into **6C Rosinante & Law.**

**Mulligan Priority: 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones,** or a combo of **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down.**

**Macro Gameplan**

Your primary win condition in this matchup relies on your **1C Board Restand Event** and/or board freeze events (**1C All Out, 3C Electrical Luna**), depending on your specific decklist. Your objective is to build a wide board and remain patient with your attacks. **Purple-Yellow Rosinante** can clear your characters in the mid-game if you rest them carelessly. Attack primarily with your leader and dispensable characters, leaving the majority of your board active to threaten the **1C Board Restand Event** from your 9 DON!! turn onward.

**General Tips**

**The Active Board Standoff**

- **Respect the Clear:** You must respect **Purple-Yellow Rosinante**'s ability to clear your board if you leave characters rested. They achieve this using their 8k attackers, **7C Sakazuki**'s power-reduction effect, and removal events like **2C Gum Gum Lightning.**
- **Patient Poking:** Attack only with your leader and expendable characters. Leave your core characters active to set up the requirement of having 5 characters with different names for your late-game **1C Board Restand Event. 4C Crocodile** is very effective here; you can attack with a 2-cost character, then use **4C Crocodile** to bounce and replay that character so it remains active during the opponent's turn.

**Psychological Pressure at 9/10 DON!!**

- By leaving your characters active as you approach your 9 and 10 DON!! turns, you force your opponent to respect the threat of a board restand. Because **Purple-Yellow Rosinante** relies on blocker characters, they will hesitate to attack with them, needing to leave defenses up in case you initiate a restand turn.
- Additionally, keeping characters active makes the opponent wary of your freeze combo (**3C Electrical Luna** plus **7C Boa** or **4C Galdino**). Even if you do not have these specific cards in hand, leaving your board active forces the opponent into difficult decisions where a single misplay can cost them the game.

**The Late-Game Loop**

From your 9 and 10 DON!! turns, initiate a sequence of poking with your leader and dispensable characters, then react based on your opponent's choices:

- **If the opponent counters and keeps blockers active:** Developing your board and passing with active characters is a perfectly fine outcome. You have reduced their hand size and can try again next turn without leaving yourself defenseless.
- **If the opponent uses their blockers:** Attack with your entire board. From here, you can either (1) use the **1C Board Restand Event** for lethal or (2) use freeze effects to lock their board and set up a lethal attack on the following turn.
- **Using 7C Gravity Blade:** If you run this card, it is an excellent tool to remove their mid-game board (preferably targeting two copies of **6C Rosinante & Law**) on the turn right before you attempt your restand lethal push.

**Key Matchup Notes**

- **Preserve Life Early:** If your opening hand is good, proactively counter out of early attacks that only require one card. You need to maintain a healthy life total to safely navigate the late-game standoff. This also helps you play around the discard effect of **4C Law**.
- **Stalling the Mid-Game:** While building your board for the 9/10 DON!! turn, you are vulnerable to high-power mid-game attacks. Use **4C Galdino** and **7C Boa** to stall their 8k characters (**6C Rosinante & Law, 7C Sakazuki**), which the opponent will otherwise swing with freely. In the late game, combine these characters with freeze events to lock down three or more attackers.
- **Baiting Counters:** If the opponent has rested characters with 6k power or less in the mid-game, attack into them. This forces a decision point where they might choose to counter instead of letting the character go. Even minor hand depletion makes it easier to read their options and outmaneuver them during the late-game standoff.
- **Respecting Counter Events:** In the late game, if the opponent leaves DON!! active, anticipate counter events like **1C I Love You** and **2C Noro Noro Beam Sword**. Attack for 7k or higher to ensure the opponent must spend at least two cards to counter, even if they use an event.

**Tech Card Considerations**

- **3C Electrical Luna / 1C All Out**: Used to freeze their board to while setting up lethal.
- **7C Gravity Blade**: For bottom-decking their 6-cost characters.

**Favorability:** Even

**Turn Order:** Go First

Going first secures the aggressive initiative and allows you to be the first player capable of threatening the **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters** on the 9 DON!! turn. Going second is also acceptable; the game will largely depend on card quality and efficient board management

**Mulligan Priority:** 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones, 4C Crocodile, or a combo of **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down**.

### Macro Gameplan

The mirror matchup revolves entirely around **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters**. The player who successfully resolves this event holds a significant advantage. Attack conservatively with your board, swinging only with characters that you can easily replace. Focus on establishing a board of five uniquely named characters to enable your own restand condition. At the same time, disrupt your opponent's setup by clearing their rested characters and using freeze effects to limit their attackers.

### General Tips

#### Managing the Core Win Condition

Your goal is to construct a board that satisfies the conditions to resolve **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters** if it is in your hand, while simultaneously respecting the threat of your opponent doing the same.

- **The Power of the Restand:** Resolving **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters** is often game-winning because this deck can quickly deplete its 2k counters when pressured by consecutive 6k attacks. Facing a wide board capable of delivering 10 to 12 attacks at a 6k minimum threshold in a single turn makes survival highly unlikely.
- **Conservative Attacking:** Swing with your characters carefully. Always consider how you will protect your board if your opponent directs all of their attacks into your rested characters. As a general rule, only attack with characters you can easily replace on subsequent turns. Avoid committing your entire board; doing so drain your hand counters during defense, or leave you exposed to consecutive plays of **3C Electrical Luna**.

#### Board Control and Positioning

- **Contesting the Board:** Only commit all of your characters to attack if your opponent has already rested their own board and you need to clear their threats. If the game is at a standstill, hold your attacks back. Instead, use your leader to pressure their life total or force out hand counters.
- **The Crocodile Loop: 4C Crocodile** is an effective tool in this standoff. You can attack your opponent for 6k power using a 2-cost character, then use **4C Crocodile** to bounce and replay that character, keeping your small assets active and safe from counter-attacks.
- **Utilizing 6k Characters:** Only attack with your 6k characters if you are prepared to defend them or if you do not mind losing them. This way, you can use them as attack magnets to protect your life total. Keeping uniquely named 6k characters active on your board is an efficient setup, as they allow you to swing for 6k without investing additional DON!! during your board restand turn.

#### Handling Opposing Rested Characters

- **Targeting Small Threats:** If the opponent leaves small characters rested (**2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**, or **1C Luffy**), attack into them to force the opponent to replace them.
- **Targeting 6k Threats:** If there are rested 6k characters on their board (**4C Crocodile, 5C Buggy**, or **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**), start your turn by poking the opponent's leader with 5k attacks. If the opponent counters out from hand, pivot your remaining attacks to their board to tax their resources. If the opponent is threatening a restand event on the following turn, prioritize clearing these rested 6k characters to disrupt their setup.
- **The Frozen Exception:** Only ignore rested characters if you can freeze them for consecutive turns using **3C Electrical Luna**. However, remain cautious: even if a character is frozen by **3C Electrical Luna**, your opponent can still restand them with their own copy of **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters**.

#### Mitigating the Enemy Restand

- **Strategic Freezing:** Another way to weaken your opponent's potential restand turn is to use the freeze effects of **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to prevent their characters from attacking. While doing this, remember that you must also continue to build your own board to threaten a restand return play.
- **Prioritizing Targets:** Your primary targets for freeze effects should be the opponent's **7C Boa** and **5C Buggy**. You should generally lower the priority on freezing their blockers (**4C Crocodile** and **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**), as the opponent is likely to keep these active to fortress up. Characters without the Blocker keyword are much more likely to be used for attacks.

#### Executing Your Restand Turn

- **The 6k Threshold:** Once you activate your restand event, your chances of winning increase significantly. Aim to swing for at least 6k power with every attack to force out 2k counters.
- **Reading the Hand:** As you sequence your 6k attacks, monitor how your opponent responds. They will eventually run out of 2k counters and begin hesitating, choosing to either take life damage, burn a counter event, or block with their 6k blockers.
- **Pivoting the Strategy:** If their hand size depletes early, you can push for lethal depending on your remaining attacks and their life count. However, if they counter heavily because they held a high volume of 2k counters, remember that you do not need to blindly commit to attacking their life. If lethal becomes impossible, pivot your remaining attacks to destroy their board instead. If needed, you can also leave your own blockers active to defend against their next turn.

#### Other Notes for the Matchup

- **The 6k Statline:** 6k power characters are the core of this matchup. You can use them as attack magnets to protect your leader's life total. Defending these characters with simple 1k counters forces your opponent to choose between over-investing to clear them or diverting their remaining attacks away from your life.
- **Evaluating Free Blocks: 4C Crocodile** and **6C Prisoner of Impel Down** are your primary blockers, and you will find opportunities to take free blocks with them throughout the game. Before resting these characters to block, evaluate how your opponent can punish the play:
  - They can use **3C Electrical Luna** or **1C All Out** to keep your rested 6k body frozen on your subsequent turn.
  - They can pivot their remaining attacks into your rested character, forcing you to drain your hand counters to keep it alive. If you can tolerate both outcomes, take the free block. If you absolutely need to preserve active attackers for your next turn, especially on the turn right before you plan to execute your board restand play, keep them active and do not get baited into blocking.
- **The 6C Prisoner of Impel Down Timing:** This character requires **5C Buggy** to gain the Blocker keyword. In the mirror match, it is generally incorrect to grant him Blocker status immediately, as your opponent can punish this by using **4C Rush Luffy** to rest him. Wait until the last possible moment to activate his blocker ability.
- **Tech Card Considerations**
  - **1C Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters:** Maximize your deck count for this card to increase your probability of drawing it and securing the win condition.
  - **7C Gravity Blade:** Highly effective for removing two of your opponent's 6k characters from the board simultaneously.
  - **3C Electrical Luna:** Used to freeze your opponent's rested characters and stall their offense.

**Favorability:** Favored

**Turn Order:** Go Second

Going second disrupts your opponent's curve, altering their on-curve transitions from **6C Shiryu**, to **8C Teach**, and finally into their **10C Teach / 8C Zehahaha** plays

**Mulligan Priority:** 1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones

### Macro Gameplan

Your objective in this matchup is straightforward: go as wide as possible and maximize your total number of attacks every turn. While **Black-Yellow Teach** possesses tools to stall and redirect your attacks, doing so forces them to expend hand cards. Commit to the board by playing two or more characters per turn, and pressure them with a barrage of 5k and 6k swings. This pressure will deplete their counter cards and lower their life cards.

### General Tips

#### Out-Curving and Out-Pacing the Opponent

This is a favored matchup for **Blue-Green Luffy**. Your goal is to play efficiently on curve and build a wide board.

- **The Curve Sequence:** Play your 1-cost and 2-cost characters in the early game. Follow this up by deploying the **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner of Impel Down** package in the mid-game. Finally, transition into **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to freeze their high-cost attackers while your wide board continues to push damage.
- **Contesting the Small Board:** If the opponent fails to develop small characters to contest yours, the matchup tilts in your favor. They will be forced to use leader attacks to clear your small characters. Even if they try, you can frequently extract value by taking free blocks with **4C Crocodile** or **6C Prisoner of Impel Down** during the early stages.
- **Wide vs. Tall Attacks:** Teach's leader ability allows them to redirect incoming attacks to their characters. Because of this, investing DON!! for large single attacks is usually incorrect unless you are making a push for lethal. Instead, prioritize multiple wide attacks over a few large ones to force out as many counter cards from their hand as possible each turn.

#### Navigating Triggers and On-KO Effects

**Black-Yellow Teach** utilizes several triggers and On KO effects that can disrupt your offensive sequence. You can play around them by structuring your attacks as follows:

- **1C Vasco Shot:** This is their most common tool. The opponent will deploy **1C Vasco Shot** to leverage their leader ability, redirecting an incoming attack to him to rest one of your characters that costs 6 or less. If they have **1C Vasco Shot** on the board, attack first with characters he is capable of resting. This forces the opponent to use their leader ability early. Once the ability is spent, you can safely adjust your remaining attacks, such as redirecting high-power swings to clear their board or targeting their life to force life takes.
- **8C Teach:** This is their strongest trigger card, capable of nullifying a small character's effects (preventing them from scaling their power) and removing one of your 5-cost characters simultaneously. Always attack with your 4/5-cost characters first. This ensures that even if they hit an **8C Teach** trigger, your 4/5-cost characters have already attacked.
- **4C San Juan Wolf:** The opponent frequently sets this card up in their life pile as a trigger. When triggered, it increases their leader's power to 7000 to defend against wide boards. If this triggers, you can counter it by attaching DON!! to your 6k or higher attackers to still match the 7000 statline. Alternatively, attack with **2C Bon Clay**, as he will automatically copy their leader's power regardless of the buff. If they have rested characters with 6k power or less, you can also pivot your smaller swings into those targets instead.
- **1C Van Augur:** This is similar to **1C Vasco Shot**, but instead of resting an attacker, his effect reduces an attacker's power by 3000. This can stop low-power attacks entirely, though you can still attach DON!! to the penalized character if you absolutely need the attack to go through. Keep this power reduction in mind when calculating your math during a lethal turn.
- **1C Doc Q:** Inclusion of this card varies by decklist. On play, it can KO two of your 1-cost characters (primarily targeting your **1C Luffy** searcher). It has a relatively low impact, but if you see it on the board, attack with your **1C Luffy** immediately to get value before it is removed.

#### Late-Game Defense and Lethal Execution

- **Managing the Life Total:** Be cautious of their late-game **8C Zehahaha**, which can take a card from your life pile. Proactively counter out of attacks to your life if your hand is healthy. Do not drop in life unnecessarily unless your hand is weak and you need the card draw. Aim to maintain a stable 3 life for as long as possible by using single-card counters from hand, then deploy **7C Boa** and **4C Galdino** to freeze their high-cost threats, such as **6C Shiryu, 8C Teach**, and **10C Teach**.
- **The 2-Life Danger Zone:** If you are at 2 life in the late game, anticipate a push from the opponent using an event play to remove your life card and push for game. They can also resolve a **10C Teach** sequence to disable one of your blockers, strip your life, and swing for the win. Account for these combinations whenever you are calculating your defenses.
- **Patience Against Hand Size:** If the opponent maintains a healthy hand size, do not rush for lethal. Continue utilizing multiple small attacks to tax their resources. They can heal their life total using cards like **5C Borsalino** and can become defensive using **10C Teach** as a blocker that disables an attacker. When you finally commit to a lethal turn, always factor in their active blockers and remember that they can redirect one of your attacks via their leader ability.
- **Tech Cards: 4C Rush Luffy:** Used to rest their late-game blockers and go for an extra attack, **7C Gravity Blade:** Useful to bottom-deck their **4C Burgess** and **1C Van Augur**.
