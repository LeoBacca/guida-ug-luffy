This 37-card core forms the foundation of the deck. It maximizes early-game consistency, mid-game swarm potential, and late-game finishing power, leaving 13 flexible slots to customize based on your preferences.

- **4x 1C Monkey.D.Luffy (Searcher):** Essential for swarming the board and finding your pieces early.
- **4x 2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones & 4x 2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay:** These low-cost units form the heart of your early game for aggression and draw power.
- **4x 5C Buggy & 8x 6C Prisoners of Impel Down:** This is your strongest play on your 5 or 6 DON!! turn. Running 8 copies of the 6C Prisoners ensures you consistently hit your mid-game power play.
- **4x 4C Galdino:** One of, if not the best Blue cards in the game. It packs stall, card draw, and the Impel Down typing into one card.
- **2x 4C Crocodile:** A powerful card for bouncing and replaying your 2-cost units. It is kept at 2 copies in the core engine to prevent hand-clogging, but you can scale it up depending on your overall counterless count.
- **2x 1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters (Restand Event), 2x 7C Boa Hancock, & 1x 6C Brook:** Your late-game finisher combo package to close out games after your early to mid-game aggression.
- **2x 2C Galdino (2k Counter):** Highly flexible. It provides a solid body to fill out your board during late-game lethal setups, or acts as a 2k counter in the worst-case scenario.

Start with this 37-card core, then fill out the remaining 13 slots with your preferred tech choices.

## Tech Choice Breakdowns

- **4x 1C Board Restand Event:** This is what makes this specific variant feel so consistent at closing out games. By maxing this out at 4 copies, I ensure that my primary win condition is almost always in my hand. I can map out my entire game plan around setting up lethal without worrying about whether I'll actually draw the event in the late game. Instead of splitting slots for defensive freeze events like **1C All Out** or **3C Electrical Luna**, I decided to go all-in on the win condition: killing the opponent before they kill me. This let me play a laser-focused game plan for 10 rounds straight and secured wins in matchups where the restand event is everything, especially the mirror match and Purple-Yellow Rosinante.
- **3x 7C Boa & 2x 6C Brook:** I bumped up the copies here to give myself a reliable back-up plan in case the board restand path gets shut down. **7C Boa** is an absolute must-have against decks that can dynamically pump their Leader's power, like Blue-Yellow Nami and Red-Blue Ace. Running 3 copies means I can reliably see at least one every single game.
- **4x 4C Crocodile:** While I only put 2 in the safety core, I firmly believe he should be a 4x inclusion, especially in this heavy restand event build. Croc is a fantastic enabler for your lethal turns because he keeps your characters active and totally safe from being attacked. Plus, having that Blocker keyword fits my late-game style perfectly whenever I need to fortress up while assembling my pieces.
- **2x 4C Ivankov:** Look, I'm a player who likes to play it risky. I will aggressively mulligan for an explosive hand rather than sitting on a mediocre one. Running 2 copies of Ivankov gives me the confidence to play with that high-risk mulligan style; his on-play search and develop effect is a literal lifesaver for fixing your hand if you happen to draw poorly.
- **2x 2C 2k Jinbe:** I wanted a clean 12 copies of 2k counters in the list, and Jinbe got the nod for two big reasons. First, just like Ivankov, he gives me the safety net to mulligan aggressively because he's a cheap 2-cost body I can drop early if I completely miss my curve. Second, he serves as a uniquely named cheap character to flood the field and turn on the restand event for late-game lethal pushes.
- **1x 4C Rush Luffy:** I love this card as a one-of surprise factor finisher or mid-game option. It's an amazing tool to have in hand when you're looking at the board and calculating your lines for the turn. In an ideal world, I'd love to run more copies, but it's a counterless card. You can't get too greedy with it or your hand will brick up heavily.
- **1x 7C Gravity Blade:** This is my personal pet card in the deck. While there are probably more "traditionally consistent" tech options out there, Gravity Blade was putting in absolute work during my testing. It allowed me to completely blow out board states where things were even or where I was slightly ahead, and it straight-up stole games against rogue decks like Red Ace or Yellow Kalgara. It's my high-risk, high-reward tech choice, and it completely caught my opponents off guard.
- **1x 0C Gum-Gum Rain:** Rounding out the 50 is a single copy of Gum-Gum Rain, which lets you pitch counterless cards to give yourself a 3k counter instead. Because this list runs a decent chunk of counterless cards, my testing showed I'd regularly end games with 1 to 3 dead bricks sitting in my hand. Having a 0-cost filter event turns those useless cards into active defense, boosting the deck's overall consistency.

### Other Techs I Would Consider

Any card from the core or control tech sections we talked about earlier is totally viable to swap in, but if I were looking to change things up, these are the top targets:

- **1C Dead Man's Game:** This is a more consistent tech option that I'd consider replacing 7C Gravity Blade with. It has incredible utility as a board control tool, a lethal finisher piece, and in the worst-case scenario, you can just use it as a 3k counter for your leader when you need to go into late-game turtle or fortress mode.
- **3C Electrical Luna / 1C All Out:** Green's signature freeze events. You can never go wrong with these if you prefer playing a more control-oriented style. I would heavily consider slotting these back in if I expected a meta heavily saturated with Purple-Yellow Rosinante, but otherwise, I feel completely fine running the deck without them.

**Where are the flex spots?** If you want to test these out and need to know what to cut from my 50-card list, the easiest flex spots to trim are: **1x 4C Crocodile, 1x 4C Rush Luffy, 1x 7C Gravity Blade, 1x 1C Board Restand Event, or 1x 0C Gum-Gum Rain.**

## What Should You Build?

What is the best build for Blue-Green Luffy? The answer to this question is exactly what I've mentioned in my previous guides: **the best list for a deck is the list that works for you!**

Copying a list one-for-one rarely works out perfectly in card games. There are just too many personal factors and shifting variables when you step into a tournament hall. You need to test, refine, and actively adjust your list depending on your own playstyle and the specific decks you expect to face on game day.

You can absolutely use my recommended 50-card tournament list as a starting point, but nothing beats a decklist that is forged from your own testing. That type of list is the only one you can pilot with full confidence and radical trust when the games get tight. Just try not to stray too far from the 37-card deck core I recommended so that you still retain the fundamental engine and playstyle of the deck.
