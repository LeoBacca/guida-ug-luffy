## Leader Ability and General Gameplan

> [IMMAGINE: banner — manga panels showing Luffy (center, smiling) flanked by other characters; a speech bubble reads "POWERS!! CERTAINLY A FORCE TO BE RECKONED WITH!!"]

### Leader Ability

The core strength of Blue-Green Luffy comes down to raw resource cheating. Your ability lets you refresh 2 active DON!! every single turn. Because the One Piece Card Game is fundamentally won or lost through board presence and combat efficiency, having these 2 extra DON!! to work with means you simply play more cards than your opponent can keep up with.

When mapping out your turns, use a simple rule of thumb: **always add 2 DON!! to your mental math.** If you are sitting on a 4-DON!! turn, your actual operational budget is 6 DON!!.

### General Gameplan

**Early-Mid Game:** Swarm the board, go wide, and use your leader ability to keep pressuring the opponent's leader. With low-cost swarmers (**1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones**) and mid-game swarmers (**5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner**), you can often keep rebuilding your board as your opponent contests your swarm.

**Late Game:** Use freeze effects from **4C Galdino** and **7C Boa** to disable the opponent's attackers, then you can finish off with late-game combo cards, the **1C Restand Event** or **Brook**.

### Early Game (0 to 4 DON!!): Swarm the Board and Aggro

Your fundamental mission in the early game is simple: **go wide with small characters (weenies) and pressure the opponent.**

**Core Early-Game Concepts & Decision Making**

- **Punishing Empty Fields:** Generally, you want to swing at the opponent's Leader with as many attacks as possible. Use your Leader ability to efficiently attach the refreshed DON!! to your small swarmers. If your opponent fails to establish their own small characters or searchers to contest yours, going aggressive early puts them on a clock. They are forced to waste their own Leader attacks clearing your small units while you continuously refill the field.
- **Managing the Mirror/Weenie Boards:** If your opponent *does* have small characters to contest yours, don't blindly swing. If you don't have hand reinforcement to replace your units once they get attacked and KO'd, consider delaying your swings. Wait for a mid-game window where you can safely protect them behind a blocker **4C Crocodile** or a **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**.
- **Managing Board Space (The Replace Rule):** If you *do* have plenty of board refill lined up for the mid-game, swing aggressively and rest your characters. This forces your opponent to waste their turn clearing them out, which opens up your board space. You never want to choke your own turns by being forced to play over your own active units. If you know a character is getting replaced anyway, **attack with it immediately.** Extract value from that card before it dies.
- **The Duplicate Sequencing Rule:** If you have multiple 2-cost characters in hand, always deploy and attack with the ones you have duplicates of first. For example, if you hold two **2C Daz Bones** and only one **2C Bentham (Mr. 2 Bon Clay)**, prioritize playing and attacking with Daz Bones. You must preserve as many uniquely named characters as possible to effortlessly enable your **1C Restand Event** requirements in the late game.
- **Daz Bones vs. Bon Clay Priority:** If you don't have duplicates and need to choose which 2-cost unit to swing with first, send **2C Daz Bones** into battle and let him take the risk of dying. **2C Bon Clay** is significantly more valuable to preserve for the late game because his effect allows him to scale and match the opponent's Leader power, ensuring he can always swing productively. In contrast, a 3k power Daz Bones can easily become completely blocked out from attacking high-power boards later on if you are forced to go low on hand cards.
- **Early-Game Searching Priorities:** Use your early searchers to secure your low-cost swarming pieces first. Once your hand is already stacked with a healthy curve of early units (1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones), pivot your search targets to find the mid-game engine pair: **5C Buggy** + a **6C Prisoner of Impel Down.**

### Optimal Turn-by-Turn Curve Plays

Let's break down exactly how you should manipulate your DON!! curve from your opening turns right into the mid-game transition, leveraging that +2 DON!! mental math to maximize your early tempo.

**1 DON!! Turn**

This opening play is completely straightforward. You drop your **1C Searcher Luffy** to fix your hand.

- **The Double-Search Line:** If you happen to already hold a second copy, or if your first search immediately grabs one, you can trigger your Leader ability to restand 1 DON!! and deploy the second **1C Luffy** right away to double-search.

**2 DON!! Turn (Operational Budget: 4 DON!!)**

With 4 total DON!! to work with thanks to your Leader ability, your sequencing is simple:

- **The Weenie Drop:** Play two 2-cost characters back-to-back. Drop the first one, use your Leader ability to restand 2 DON!!, and then drop the second. You can also weave in any remaining 1C Searcher Luffys here.
- **Enforcing the Duplicate Rule:** Remember to drop your duplicate characters first. You want to get them on the board so you can aggressively attack and sacrifice them early, keeping your uniquely named characters completely safe for the late-game restand requirement.

**3 DON!! Turn (Operational Budget: 5 DON!!)**

Your general blueprint for this turn is to spend 4 DON!! developing two 2-cost characters, leaving your final 1 DON!! to push an attack.

- **The Luffy Pump Sequencing:** If you have a 1C Searcher Luffy active from Turn 1, you can execute an efficient combat line. First, swing with your Leader for 5k. Then, play a 2-cost character and use your Leader ability to restand your DON!!. Now, attach the remaining DON!! to your active 1C Searcher Luffy. Because you have two uniquely named characters on the board, Luffy's base power rises to 2k, meaning those attached DON!! let you swing with him for a 5k attack.
- **The Draw-First Check:** If you have 2-cost units in hand that draw cards on play (like Daz Bones), *always* play them out before you declare your Leader attack. You want to see if they draw you into a 1C Searcher Luffy. If they don't, you can safely pump your Leader attack to 6k. If they do, you can immediately drop the newly drawn Luffy to search the top of your deck before committing your combat steps.

### Mid Game (5 to 8 DON!!): Pressuring and Setting Up the Board

In the mid game, your primary objective is to continuously pressure your opponent with high-volume attacks while aggressively developing your board.

How you approach these mid-game turns dictates everything. It depends entirely on **which specific lethal finishers you are currently holding in your hand** or, if your hand is dry, how many copies are left in your deck to search out. Your game plan splits into two distinct strategic pathways depending on your win condition:

> [IMMAGINE: two proxy cards — cost 1, "Let's Go!! To The Navy Headquarters!" (the Restand Event); and cost 6, power 6000, "Brook".]

**Pathway A: The 1C Board Restand Event Plan**

If you are holding the **1C Restand Event**, your goal is to assemble **5 uniquely named characters** on your field and keep them completely safe in preparation for a lethal combo on your 9 or 10 DON!! turn.

- **Play Conservatively:** You must be careful with your swings. Only attack with a character if you can immediately replace them if they get KO'd, or if you have the necessary counter power in hand to protect them on the swing back.
- **Prioritizing Your Attacks:** If you need to exert pressure, filter your attacks through this priority list:
  - Swing with characters you hold **duplicates** of in hand, since they are easily replaced if they die.
  - Swing with small units that require DON!! attachments *only* if you can immediately drop a bigger, uniquely named character to fill their space if they get cleared.
  - Swing with your larger bodies *only* if your hand is packed with enough counter cards to safely guard them.
- **The Patient Math (1 vs. 2 Attacks):** If you attack with a character early and they get cleared by a counter-attack, you only extracted 1 attack's worth of value from that card. However, if you stay patient and leave them active on the board, that same card will give you **2 attacks in a single turn** once you trigger your restand event. Always ask yourself: *"Will leaving these characters active win me the game on the very next turn?"* If the answer is yes, keep them active.
- **Smart Countering:** Guarding your life efficiently means throwing away the right cards. Low-cost characters are your lifeblood for enabling the restand event if your board gets thinned out. **Never discard uniquely named cheap characters as counter bait** if you need them to flood the field on your final turn. Instead, pitch duplicates or heavy, expensive tech cards that you won't have the DON!! to cast during your explosive endgame push.

**Pathway B: The 6C Brook Plan**

If you are holding **6C Brook** as your designated finisher, your strategy shifts from a patient setup into a relentless, hyper-aggressive clock. You need to actively force your opponent down to 0 or 1 life card as fast as humanly possible.

- **Force the Life Takes:** Fire off attacks at **minimum of 7k power** to force them into taking life cards or bleeding multiple counter cards from their hand.
- **The Hunt for 7C Boa Hancock:** Dig aggressively through your deck using your searchers to find **7C Boa Hancock**. She is the absolute best pairing for Brook. As the highest base-power attacker in your list, her unblockable keyword ensures that she extracts maximum value when Brook chooses her to restand for a second swing.
- **Alternative Restand Targets:** If you can't find Boa, your 6k power bodies (like your 6C Prisoners) are excellent secondary targets. Additionally, **2C Bon Clay** is an alternative target to restand if you are fighting against Leaders that pump their own power levels (like Blue-Yellow Nami or Red-Blue Ace), since he will copy their boosted stats for both swings.

### Mid-Game Curve Plays (5 to 8 DON!!)

Because your Leader ability hands you an extra 2 active DON!! every single turn, your operational budget in the mid-game gives you an immense amount of tactical flexibility. Let's map out your optimal patterns for each threshold.

**5 DON!! Turn (Operational Budget: 7 DON!!)**

- **Option A: The Buggy Swarm (The Premier Line)**
  - Play **5C Buggy**, triggering his on-play effect to cheat out a **6C Prisoner of Impel Down**. This immediately floods your board with two 6k power bodies. From there, use your Leader ability to restand 2 DON!! to either push extra combat pressure or deploy another 2-cost unit from your hand.
  - This is hands-down the most explosive play you can make on 5 DON!!. It establishes an overwhelming wall of high-stat characters that are difficult for decks like Purple Enel and Blue-Yellow Nami to deal with.

  - Attach 1 DON!! to an active 2-cost character and swing. Then, spend your remaining 4 DON!! to drop **4C Crocodile**, bouncing that exact rested 2-cost unit back to your hand and replaying it. Use your Leader ability to restand 2 DON!! to expand your board or push further attacks.
  - This line is phenomenal because it exerts aggressive combat pressure while keeping your cheap units active and perfectly shielded from your opponent's counter-attacks. You cycle your hand with a fresh draw, develop a 6k blocker, and flush a counterless brick out of your hand.

- **Option C: The Ivankov Pivot**
  - Drop **4C Emporio Ivankov** to go wide, restand 2 DON!!, and allocate those resources into board development or extra swings. Only settle for this if you completely missed your Buggy and Crocodile pieces, as Ivankov's lower base power makes it the weakest of the three standard lines.
- **Emergency Management:** If you lack all of the above options, you must lean into emergency plays: flood the field with multiple 2-cost units, drop a **4C Rush Luffy** to force immediate aggro, or park a standalone **4C Galdino** for later. If you have card-drawing 2-cost units like Bon Clay or Daz Bones, **play them first at the very start of the turn,** they might just draw you right into a 5C Buggy or 4C Crocodile to save your curve.

**6 DON!! Turn (Operational Budget: 8 DON!!)**

This turn operates identically to your 5 DON!! turn, with one advantage: you have an extra DON!! to play with.

- **The Buggy Priority:** Your absolute best play remains **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner**, but you now have an extra DON!! to pump into higher combat numbers or to drop a utility piece like **1C Searcher Luffy**.
- **The Double 4C Drop:** Alternatively, you can spend your full budget to develop two 4-cost characters. This looks like playing **two 4C Crocodiles** to establish double 6k blockers while cycling your hand, dropping a **Croc + Ivankov** pairing to rapidly spread your board wide, or playing a **Croc** and parking a **4C Galdino**.
  - **The Downside:** Spending all 8 DON!! on pure character deployment means your attacks this turn will be strictly limited to units that don't require DON!! attachments (essentially just your Leader and any Crocodile you established on the prior turn).

This is the most critical junction of the match. If you can successfully maintain a board of **5 uniquely named characters,** you officially begin playing a psychological war against your opponent. From this point onward, you are actively threatening to end the game on any given turn with your Restand Event.

Here are your standard power plays for these high-resource turns:

- **Line 1: The 7C Boa Hancock Lockdown**

  - Play **7C Boa Hancock,** then restand 2 DON!! to either push an attack or deploy a 2-cost unit. This is an oppressive play because Boa's on-play effect completely freezes an opposing attacker, buying you the exact window of time you need to set up your late-game win condition. Furthermore, it establishes a **9k unblockable body** that will violently bleed cards or life from your opponent every single time she swings.

These splits are perfect for aggressively tailoring your 5-name requirements. If you have your **2C Galdino (2k Counter)** in hand, this is the prime window to develop him onto the board to make your upcoming lethal turns exponentially more explosive.

### Late Game (9 DON!!+) - Finishers and Lethal Combos

The late game is where this deck shines because you have **11 to 12 usable DON!!** to work with every single turn. This resource pool gives you flexibility. You can use your DON!! to replace fallen characters, pump up your attackers, drop big finisher combos, or leave it active to hold up counter events on defense.

Look at your hand and assess the board state without locking yourself into one line of play. Here are the primary routes you can take in the late game:

**The Finisher Lines (Closing the Game)**

  - **When to use it:** The opponent's resources are low, and they are down to few life cards.

Then, spend 7 DON!! to play your Restand Event and refresh your whole board. Use your Leader ability to restand 2 more DON!!, attach them to your newly active attackers, and push again.

**The Turtle Lines (Stalling & Leaving Counter Up)**

If you can't afford a heavy 7-cost play and need to save DON!! for counter events, you should pivot to a defensive stall game:

- **The First Action Rule:** The best way to start a stall turn is to **play 4C Galdino first and trash him immediately to freeze a threat and draw 2 cards.** This gives you fresh hand information to plan the rest of your turn.
- **Allocating the Rest:** With your 8 remaining DON!!, you can go for a **4+4 play** (like double 4C Crocodiles), or play one Crocodile, use 2 DON!! for attacks, and leave 2 DON!! active to hold up your counter events.

**The Bottom Line**

At 10 DON!!+, you effectively have **12 usable resources** to play with. Avoid tunnel vision. Always look at your hand, map out all the math, and look for the line that gives you the highest value.

### Restand Event Fundamentals

**1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters**

**1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** (which I usually call the **1C Restand Event** or **Board Restand Event**) is the absolute defining win condition for Blue-Green Luffy's Impel Down archetype.

To activate it, you must have **5 characters with different names on board.** It is a strict, demanding condition that requires active setup from Turn 1, but the payoff is game-ending: it restands your entire board, all your characters and your Leader. This lets you swing at your opponent up to a maximum of **12 times in a single turn.** Because of the deck's early-to-mid-game aggro potential, resolving this event almost always guarantees a win.

**How Many Copies?**

While the count varies among players, I have tested every iteration of this deck: running 0, 1, 2, 3, and a maximum of 4 copies. From my testing, **the 4-copy variant is the most successful.**

- **Why 4 copies?** I want to consistently threaten and execute this turn by my 9 or 10 DON!! turn every single game. Maxing it out ensures I always draw it, allowing me to map out my entire playstyle around this win condition.
- **The Worst-Case Scenario:** Even if your board gets answered and you can't hit your 5 different names, it isn't a dead card. It doubles as a **3k counter** for your Leader during fortress or turtle scenarios.
- **The Trap of Running Fewer Copies:** If you only run 2 copies, you will regularly find yourself in spots where you successfully built the perfect 5-name board, but you lack the actual event card in hand to close out the game. I highly recommend testing the full 4 copies while you are learning the fundamentals of the deck.

**Setting Up the Board Restand (Early to Mid-Game)**

Hitting that 5-name requirement on your 9 or 10 DON!! turn requires intentional planning throughout the match. Keep these three core concepts in mind:

1. **The Duplicate Sacrifice Rule**

You must play and attack with your duplicate characters first. If you hold two copies of Daz Bones and one Bon Clay, Daz Bones goes into battle first. You want your opponent to target and KO your duplicates so that your remaining field naturally consists of uniquely named units for the late game.

2. **Tactical Counter Discarding**

When protecting your life, do not just toss cards blindly. Filter your counter choices through these rules:

- **Pitch Duplicates First:** Discard cards you have multiples of, or heavy pieces you won't have the DON!! to cast late game.
- **Pitch Broken Combos:** Throw away **5C Buggy** if your hand has no 6C Prisoners left, or dump **6C Prisoners** if you have no way to cheat them out.
- **Keep Your Enablers:** Hold onto low-cost units like **1C Searcher Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, and 2C Daz Bones** in your hand. If your opponent manages to clear part of your board, these cheap, uniquely named enablers are what you will drop to flood the field right before you trigger the event.

3. **Practicing Board Patience**

If you have successfully established a healthy setup of 3 to 5 differently named characters, **consider leaving them active.** Do not give your opponent free targets to swing into. Only commit to an attack if:

- You absolutely must contest their board or apply pressure.
- You can immediately replace that specific character from your hand if they die.
- You have enough counter power to protect them while they are rested.
- **The Golden Rule:** If dropping your restand event on the *following* turn wins you the match, skip your attacks today. Protect your board so you can strike twice with every single unit tomorrow.

### The "5 DON!!" Concept

While starting with 5 characters is the best-case scenario, it rarely happens against competitive opponents. More often, you will start your turn with 3 or 4 characters and need to drop pieces from your hand to fill out your board before casting the event.

To do this successfully, you must master **Leader Ability Math**.

By your 10 DON!! turn, your Leader's ability effectively gives you 2 extra resources, meaning **you actually have 12 DON!! to work with.** Since the Board Restand Event strictly costs 7 DON!!, you are left with exactly **5 extra DON!!** to play the game.

These 5 extra DON!! are your entire budget for the turn, and they must be split between two categories:

- **Mandatory DON!! Requirements:** This includes the cost of playing a 4th or 5th character from your hand to hit your 5-name threshold, as well as mandatory attachments for characters that require a DON!! to swing (like Bon Clay, Daz Bones, or Rush Luffy).
- **Flex DON!! Attachments:** Any leftover resources from those 5 DON!! are purely extra, allowing you to pump your characters for larger attacks.

Let's look at how to apply this 5 DON!! budget to common mid-game board states.

### When Should You Use the Restand Event? (The Combat Math)

Instead of guessing if your opponent can survive, you can use a straightforward way to check for guaranteed lethal. You just need to compare the total number of attacks you have against the total number of defensive resources your opponent has left.

**1. Know Your Attack Maximums**

- **Start with 5 Characters + Leader:** 12 Total Attacks
- **Start with 4 Characters + Leader:** 10 Total Attacks
- **Start with 3 Characters + Leader:** 8 Total Attacks

**2. Count the Enemy's Defensive Resources**

Look across the table and calculate their total defense pool:

**Opponent's Defensive Resources = Hand Cards + Remaining Life + Active Blockers**

If you have 12 attacks lined up and your opponent's combined Hand + Life + Blockers equals 9, **you have mathematically secured lethal.** Barring extreme trigger luck, they physically do not have enough cards to stop you.

**3. Reading the Nuance**

- **Watch for Counter Strain:** Pay attention to how they handled mid-game pokes. If they are burning valuable counter events early or taking life because they can't handle repetitive 6k swings, their hand is likely choked with "bricks" (non-counter characters). Strike now. Game knowledge of knowing how much counter is commonly in your opponent's deck is also useful in making reads.
- **Respect Defensive Triggers:** Against Yellow (4C Perona disables an attacker), Green (3C Electrical Luna resting an attacker), or Blue (7C Gravity Blade bottom decking a unit), a single removal Trigger can ruin your math. If a removal trigger will break your 5-name requirement mid-turn, save your DON!! instead of pre-attaching. Keep extra DON!! open so you can drop a cheap 2-cost backup unit from your hand to fix the board on the fly.

### Other Restand Event Concepts

**The Non-Lethal Restand (Pivoting)**

You do not always have to use the Restand Event to end the game. It is a tempo tool that can be used defensively:

- **The Mid-Turn Pivot:** If your opponent reveals an unexpected flood of 2k counters and lethal becomes impossible, pivot your remaining attacks into their rested characters to wipe their board, or leave your own blockers active for defense.
- **The Defensive Board Wipe:** If you are staring down an overwhelming enemy board, resolve the Restand Event purely to swing twice at their characters. This completely resets their aggressive momentum so you don't die on their return turn.

**Playing to Your Outs in Desperate Spots**

When you are behind, you must take calculated risks to assemble your combo pieces:

- **Fishing via Life:** If you have 4 copies of the Restand Event in your deck but don't have it in hand, deliberately take life damage to try and draw into it.
- **Aggressive Digging:** If you lack the 5 unique names, spend your early DON!! playing your searchers (1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Daz Bones, 4C Ivankov) first to aggressively sculpt the missing pieces of your board before declaring attacks.

**The 2C 2k Galdino Interaction**

2C 2k Galdino is potent to play as your 5th character to fill the board because his rested-DON attachment effect can be used **twice**: once when you play him, and a second time after the Restand Event untaps him.

**Crucial Math Warning:** Galdino rests himself to attach up to 4 rested DON!! per activation. On your first activation, **you must attach a maximum of 3 rested DON!!.** If you attach all 4, you will only have 6 active DON!! remaining, leaving you unable to cast your 7-cost Restand Event.

**When to Abandon the Restand Strategy**

You do not need to win with the Restand Event every match. Against heavy removal decks like Purple Enel, keeping 5 characters alive is almost impossible.

In these matchups, accept that the Restand Event is simply a **3k counter** to protect your Leader. Shift your win condition to individual combo pieces like **7C Boa, 6C Brook** and **4C Rush Luffy** to win through steady aggression. ### Other Offensive, Defensive, and Setup Concepts

**Capitalizing on Hand Reads**

As an aggro deck, you will be poking your opponent with a high volume of attacks throughout the game. This constant pressure allows you to observe how they counter and make reads on their hand composition.

- **Reading Overcounters:** If you attack into a threshold that only requires a 1k counter, but your opponent defends with a 2k counter, they are overcountering. This tells you two things: either they have no 1k counters left (meaning their hand is purely 2k counters or uncounterable bricks), or they are holding 1k counters that they refuse to discard because they plan to play those characters. In this scenario, keep spamming attacks that only ask for 1k counters. Adding an extra DON!! to force a 2k counter is a waste of resources if they are already dropping 2k counters against your smallest attacks.
- **Reading a Lack of 2k Counters:** If you present an attack that requires a 2k counter and the opponent hesitates heavily, takes life at a dangerously low threshold, or is forced to burn a counter event, they likely lack 2k counters. This read becomes even more reliable if a high volume of their 2k counters are already visible in the trash. When you spot this weakness, maximize your pressure by consistently hitting thresholds that demand 2k counters to force them into game losing decisions.

**The Late-Game Defensive Pivot**

Despite its multi-attack potential, this deck can play a slow, defensive game. When a clear lethal push is not secured, forcing it is often how you lose games you could have won.

### 4C Crocodile

Crocodile provides excellent board management and hand optimization.

- **Protecting Weak Units:** Attack with your small characters first, then use Crocodile's effect to bounce them to your hand and replay them. This keeps them active and safe from being attacked.
- **Hand Filtering:** If your hand is clogged with cards that have no counter value, use Crocodile to swap them. For example, swing with **2C Daz Bones** (a 1k counter), bounce him to your hand, and replay a counterless card like **2C Bon Clay** instead. This moves a defensive counter card back into your hand. You can do the same trick by bouncing **2C Galdino** or **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** when you strictly need a 2k counter.
- **DON!! Multiplication:** You can use Crocodile to bounce and replay **2C Galdino**, allowing you to use Galdino's DON!! attachment effect twice in a single turn to buff your 6k attackers.

### 4C Galdino

Galdino functions as a primary tool for slowing down opponents while cycling your deck.

- **Patience with the Freeze:** You do not have to use his trash effect immediately if there are no high-threat targets to freeze. - **Information First:** As a reminder, if you do plan to use Galdino's effect on your turn, always do it as your absolute first action. Drawing that card early gives you the information you need to plan the rest of your turn.

### 2C Galdino 2k

## Mulligan and Search Fundamentals

Managing your hand effectively is the key to maintaining the relentless board pressure that defines this deck.

### General Mulligan Strategy

Your mulligan is simple: prioritize speed and board presence. You want to start the game by playing two 1-2 cost characters on your very first turn.

- **Priority Targets:** Look specifically for your 1-2 cost enablers: **1C Searcher Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, and 2C Daz Bones.**
- **The Follow-up:** If your hand already includes at least two of the cards above, keep cards that guarantee your mid-game tempo: **4C Crocodile,** the **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoner** package, or **4C Ivankov.**
- **Matchup Adjustment:** Against Leaders that can boost their power to 7k (such as **Blue-Yellow Nami**), prioritize **2C Bon Clay, 4C Crocodile,** and the **Buggy/Prisoner package.** You need these higher-stat units to effectively attack into their boosted statline.

### Searching Fundamentals

When you resolve your search effects (like **1C Luffy** or **4C Ivankov**), your choice depends entirely on what phase of the game you are in and which pieces are missing from your hand:

- **If you don't have low-cost units yet:** Prioritize getting your early game pieces: **1C Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, and 2C Daz Bones.**
- **If you already have your low-cost units:** Prioritize getting your mid-game bridges: **5C Buggy + 6C Prisoners, or 4C Crocodile.**
- **If your early and mid-game are set:** Prioritize securing your late-game win conditions and combo pieces: **7C Boa, or the 1C Restand Event.**
- **If you are lacking diverse names on the board:** Prioritize cheap characters you haven't played yet to fill out the 5-name requirement: **1C Luffy, 2C Daz Bones, 2C Bon Clay, 2C Jinbe, or 2C Galdino.**
- **Strategy Tip:** When searching, always assess your board first. If you are lacking the unique names required for your late-game Restand Event, grab cheap backup units to flood the field rather than holding out for a mid-game bridge piece. Filling the board with diverse names is the most important step toward your win condition.
