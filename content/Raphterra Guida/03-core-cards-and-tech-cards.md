## The Early-Game Swarm Army

> [IMMAGINE: Three-card row on a green/blue radar background. Card 1: cost 1, power 0, "Monkey.D.Luffy". Card 2: cost 2, power 2000, "Mr.1(Daz Bones)". Card 3: cost 2, power 1000, "Mr.2.Bon.Kurei(Bentham)".]

Blue-Green Luffy applies intense board pressure very early in the match while maintaining hand size. This engine is driven by three low-cost characters (commonly referred to as "weenies") that replace themselves with a draw on play while threatening a minimum base of 5k or 6k attacks: **1C Searcher Luffy, 2C Bon Clay, and 2C Daz Bones.**

You should automatically run **4 copies of each**. They maximize your mulligan consistency, and drawing multiple copies can outright win matchups against opponents who fail to stabilize against early aggression.

**The Strategic Value of "Pseudo-Blockers"**

What makes these characters great is their utility after attacking. Once rested, your opponent is forced to attack into them. If they ignore them, you get another high-value, cheap attack next turn. Because of this, they function as **pseudo-blockers**, absorbing attacks that would have otherwise gone directly to your Life. This creates card advantage over the course of a game, especially if your opponent lacks low-cost characters of their own to clear yours.

Additionally, these low-cost units are the primary tools used to flood your board to 5 characters in the late game, enabling your **Board Restand Event** lethal combos.

### 1C Luffy Searcher (OP16-034)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Monkey.D.Luffy", cost 1, power 0, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[DON!!x1] [Your Turn] This Character gains +1000 power for each of your Characters with a different card name. [On Play] Look at 3 cards from the top of your deck, reveal up to 1 [Impel Down] type card and add it to your hand. Then, place the rest at the bottom of your deck in any order." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Straw Hat Crew. OP16-034.]

**Role:** Searcher, Early Aggressor, Restand Combo Enabler, Pseudo-Blocker

The primary searcher of the deck. When 1 DON!! is attached, it gains 1k power for each character with a different name on your field. Because this deck swarms effortlessly with cards like **5C Buggy, 6C Prisoner,** and **4C Ivankov**, this 1C unit easily reaches 5k or 6k attack thresholds.

- **Curve-Enabler:** Search for cards that are missing from your hand to fill out your early-to-mid-game curve. If you lack other early characters, prioritize searching for **2C Bon Clay** or **2C Daz Bones**. If your early hand is already strong, prioritize finding **5C Buggy** and **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** to secure your mid-game transitions.
- **Early-Game Sequencing:** Always prioritize attacking with **1C Luffy** first during your early turns. This locks in his power boost while your board has maximum name diversity. Since he has already provided value as a searcher and an attacker, you can freely let him be cleared by your opponent after resting.
- **Late-Game Finisher Setup:** As the cheapest character in the deck, he is your most resource-efficient tool for flooding the board with unique names to enable your **1C Board Restand Event** lethal turns.

### 2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones (OP16-054)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Mr.1 (Daz.Bonez)", cost 2, power 2000, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[DON!!x1] [Your Turn] If you have 5 or more cards in your hand, this Character gains +3000 power. [On Play] Draw 1 card." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Former Baroque Works. OP16-054.]

**Role**: Hand Cycle, Early Swarm, Early Aggressor, Restand Event Enabler, Pseudo-Blocker

A vital unit used to swarm the board early while drawing a card to maintain resources. He can always attack for 6k power as long as you maintain 5 or more cards in your hand, making him an efficient threat during your opening turns.

- **Hand Size Management:** Always keep his 5-card hand condition in mind, especially in the late game when your resources might be depleted. It is a common mistake to forget that **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones** loses his 6k attack capability once your hand drops below this threshold.
- **Lethal Setup Resource Allocation:** When setting up your Board Restand Event lethal turns, factor in your hand size before committing resources. If your hand is depleted and Daz Bones cannot hit the 6k threshold anyway, the DON!! you would have attached to him should be allocated to your other characters instead.

### 2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay (OP16-055)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Mr.2.Bon.Kurei(Bentham)", cost 2, power 1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[On Play] Draw 1 card. [DON!!x1] [When Attacking] This Character's base power becomes the same as your opponent's Leader's power during this turn." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Former Baroque Works. OP16-055.]

**Role**: Hand Cycle, Anti-High Power Leader, Early Swarm, Early Aggressor, Restand Event Enabler, Pseudo-Blocker

Functions similarly to **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones** by swarming the field and drawing a card on play. He copies the opposing Leader's power when attacking, allowing him to act as a natural 6k attacker against standard matchups while scaling effortlessly against higher-power threats.

- **Counter-Forcing Pressure:** Because he copies the opponent's Leader power, his base attacks always require at least a 2k counter to block. Attaching 2 DON!! to him scales his power even further, forcing the opponent to commit either two cards or a counter event to stop the damage.
- **Matchup Scaling Tactics:** Against Leaders with higher base stats (such as 6k base Leaders like Red-Blue Ace) or Leaders that self-boost via effects (such as Blue-Yellow Nami), save **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** for the mid-to-late game. His scaling attacks become difficult to stop in the late game, so avoid sacrificing him early in these specific matchups.
- **The Leader Buff Exploit:** A great trick to keep in mind is that **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** copies the opponent's Leader's power regardless of which card he is actually attacking. In niche scenarios where your opponent's Leader's power is heavily boosted, you can choose to attack a lower-power character on their board instead, using their own Leader's buff against them to secure an efficient character clear.

## Mid-Game Enablers

> [IMMAGINE: Five-card row on a radar background. Card 1: cost 4, power 6000, "Crocodile". Card 2: cost 5, power 6000, "Buggy". Card 3: cost 6, power 6000, "Prisoner of Impel Down". Card 4: cost 4, power 4000, "Emporio.Ivankov". Card 5: cost 4, power 5000, "Mr.3(Galdino)".]

While your low-cost army establishes early pressure, the mid-game is where Blue-Green Luffy cements its board state and transitions toward its late-game win conditions.

The mid-game units in this deck focus heavily on resource efficiency and tempo. Through high-value blockers, chain deployments, and hand-replenishing utility, these cards ensure you maintain momentum. Most importantly, they allow you to keep a wide, diverse board of unique character names. This maximizes the utility of your Leader ability and sets up the exact requirements needed for your game-ending restand event turns.

### 4C Crocodile (OP16-045)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Crocodile", cost 4, power 6000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[Blocker] [On Play] You may return 1 of your Characters with a cost of 2 or more to the owner's hand: Play up to 1 [Impel Down] type Character card with a cost of 2 or less from your hand." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Former Baroque Works. OP16-045.]

**Role**: Hand Cycler and Refill, Efficient Blocker, Restand Event Setup

While **4C Crocodile** lacks a counter value and can feel like a brick initially, it is an essential piece of your strategy, particularly for variants heavily focused on the **1C Board Restand Event**.

- **The Bounce and Replay Loop:** The primary usage of **4C Crocodile** is to bounce and replay your **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** or **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones** after they have already attacked. This returns your 2C unit to an active position while immediately triggering their "On Play" draw effects. You effectively reset an attacker, maintain your board presence, and net card neutral development, all while locking in a wide board of diverse character names for your restand combos.
- **Early Blocker Efficiency:** Deploying a 6k blocker early is efficient, especially against standard 5k power Leaders. If an opponent wants to clear your rested early-game units, they are forced to commit at least 6k power to their attacks just to prevent **4C Crocodile** from granting you a free block.
- **Anti-Buff Safety Valve:** This bounce mechanic is exceptional when paired with **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** against Leaders that can self-buff **(Blue-Yellow Nami)**. You can swing with Bon Clay for scaling power, then bounce and replay him to keep him active and safe from being targeted by attacks on the opponent's turn.
- **Late-Game Defensive Utility:** In the late game, **4C Crocodile** shifts into a defensive wall. You can also use his effect to bounce your own 2k counters, such as **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** or **2C Jinbe**, back to your hand if you urgently need to build up your counters. Note that you can only target characters that cost 2C or higher, meaning you cannot bounce **1C Luffy** to cycle his search effect.

**Niche Combos**

- **2C Galdino (ST30):** Use his Activate Main ability to rest him and attach 4 rested DON!!, then bounce and replay him with **4C Crocodile** to trigger his Activate Main a second time in the same turn.
- **2C Rest Galdino (OP16):** Play him to rest an opposing character, attack into it, and then bounce and replay him with **4C Crocodile** to rest a second threat. This provides incredibly efficient early-to-mid-game board clears.

When I first started playing this deck, I thought that 4C Crocodile was just an average card mainly because its a brick, but as I played the deck more, I realized how important its role is in setting up your board presence to ensrue you can keep attacking every single turn, and most importantly, to setup your board for a strong turn where you can use your board restand event.

**Recommendation:** Run 3 to 4 copies. Do not underestimate its role in sustaining your offensive momentum and establishing the perfect setup for a game-ending restand turn.

### 5C Buggy (OP16-048) + 6C Prisoners (OP16-042)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Buggy", cost 5, power 6000, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[On Play] If your Leader has the [Impel Down] type, draw 1 card and play up to 1 [Prisoner of Impel Down] card from your hand. [Once Per Turn] This effect can be activated when your opponent attacks: Up to 1 of your [Prisoner of Impel Down] cards gains [Blocker] during this turn." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Buggy Pirates. OP16-048.]

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Prisoner of Impel Down", cost 6, power 6000, #COUNTER +2000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "Under the rules of this game, you may have any number of this card in your deck." CHARACTER. Impel Down. OP16-042.]

**Role:** Mid-to-Late Game Swarm, Restand Event Enabler

In most scenarios, chaining **5C Buggy** into **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** is the strongest possible play on your 5 DON!! or 6 DON!! turn. This combination represents a powerful on-curve board development. Following your early-game swarm, it reinforces your presence by dropping two 6k power bodies onto the field at once, one of which can act as a blocker that can get free blocks in the early game.

- **Setting Up the Restand Win Condition:** Playing this in the mid-game positions you for a late-game lethal push. Because it immediately deploys two characters with distinct names, it satisfies a significant portion of your restand requirements. Between your 5 DON!! and 8 DON!! turns, combining **5C Buggy** and **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** alongside one or two lower-cost units makes setting up a **1C Board Restand Event** turn by 9 or 10 DON!! straightforward.
- **Recovery Against Removal Decks:** This engine is critical for recovering against decks capable of wiping out your early low-cost characters, such as Yellow leaders utilizing **5C Wyper**. If your initial board gets cleared, this duo allows you to re-establish a threatening presence instantly. In matchups where your characters are removed every single turn, like Purple Enel, opponents struggle to clean up both a 5C and a 6C body simultaneously, ensuring at least one 6k threat sticks to the board.
- **Efficiency Against High-Power Leaders:** This combination shines against Leaders with 6k power or more. Having natural 6k bodies means you can pressure the opposing Leader without needing to invest extra DON!! attachments. This is vital on your restand combo turns, as it frees up your active DON!! to be allocated toward boosting your smaller characters. For example, against **Blue-Yellow Nami** when she boosts herself to a 7k power Leader, you only need to attach a single DON!! to these 6k bodies to start efficiently forcing cards out of her hand.

**Recommendation:** **5C Buggy** is a mandatory 4-of core card. **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** is a unique card that bypasses standard deck-building limits. In my testing, running exactly 8 copies of **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** provides the ideal consistency for drawing into this mid-game power spike.

### 4C Galdino (OP16-056)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Mr.3(Galdino)", cost 4, power 5000, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[Activate: Main] You may trash this Character: Draw 2 cards, and up to 1 of your opponent's Characters with a cost of 9 or less cannot attack until the end of your opponent's next End Phase." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Former Baroque Works. OP16-056.]

**Role**: Aggression Stall, Hand Refill, High-Value Utility

**4C Galdino** is one of the premier Blue utility cards in the format due to the immense value it generates for a 4 DON!! investment. For 4 DON!!, it stops an opponent's character costing 9 or less from attacking, which can mitigate significant pressure depending on the attack of the target (usually worth 2-3 cards worth of counter). Additionally, he can trash himself to draw 2 cards, providing substantial hand advantage while digging through your deck for key resources.

- **Leader Ability Synergy:** Thanks to our Leader's ability to restand 2 DON!!, **4C Galdino** effectively functions as a 2 DON!! play. For a net cost of 2 DON!!, you freeze an opponent's boss monster and draw 2 cards, leaving the remainder of your DON!! active to extend your plays. It pairs exceptionally well with development tools like **5C Buggy** and **6C Prisoners of Impel Down**, allowing you to stall the opponent's offense while simultaneously expanding your board and hand size.
- **Delayed Activation Value:** You do not always need to activate **4C Galdino**'s stall and draw effect immediately. If your opponent lacks removal options to clear it, you can leave it on the board, attack with it on the following turn, and then trash it afterward to activate its effect. This extracts maximum value from a single card. If you lack a strong play on your 4 DON!! turn, you can simply deploy **4C Galdino** and let it sit until the turn its effect becomes most efficient.

**Recommendation:** This is one of the strongest individual cards in the deck. It should always be run at 4 copies.

### 4C Ivankov (OP16-026)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Emporio.Ivankov", cost 4, power 4000, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[On Play] Look at 3 cards from the top of your deck, reveal up to 1 [Impel Down] type card, add it to your hand and place the rest at the bottom of your deck in any order. Then, play up to 1 Character card with a cost of 2 or less from your hand." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Revolutionary Army. OP16-026.]

**Role**: Secondary Searcher, Mid-Game Go-Wide Tool, Hand Fixer, Restand Event Enabler

This card presents a classic love-hate relationship. The primary strength of **4C Ivankov** lies strictly on your 4 DON!! turn. Playing it here allows you to search for a card and potentially deploy **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** or **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones** alongside it. If you lack a solid opening hand with multiple early units, **4C Ivankov** searches for those missing pieces and allows you to build a wider board, heavily improving your early-game consistency after a rough mulligan.

- **Late-Game Diminishing Returns:** Outside of that specific 4 DON!! turn, **4C Ivankov** loses a lot of value and often sits in hand as a basic 1k counter. With a base statline of only 4k power, it requires you to attach 1 DON!! just to threaten a standard attack. In the mid-to-late game, your DON!! is usually better spent developing larger threats or dropping your 2C units directly to widen your board.
- **The Combo Utility Exception:** The saving grace for **4C Ivankov** in the late game is its ability to act as a two-in-one deployment that puts two distinct character names on the field simultaneously. This makes it a useful tool for setting up your **1C Board Restand Event** combo turns when you need to quickly hit your unique name count.

**Recommendation:** Your exact copy count depends entirely on how much you value early-game consistency versus open slots for tech options. Running 2 copies is a reliable baseline, but it is one of the first cards to consider cutting when making room for specific tech choices.

## Late-Game Combo Finishers

> [IMMAGINE: Four-card row on a radar background. Card 1: cost 7, power 9000, "Boa Hancock". Card 2: cost 6, power 6000, "Brook". Card 3: cost 1, "Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters!". Card 4: cost 4, power 6000, "Monkey.D.Luffy".]

After establishing your board and applying pressure during the early and mid-game, these high-cost cards and combo pieces are used to close out the match.

The finishers in this deck focus on bypassing blockers, pushing unblockable damage, and restanding characters to attack multiple times. Because your early aggression often forces opponents to use up their counters, these late-game tools are designed to push through final points of damage when the opponent's hand resources are low.

### 7C Boa Hancock (OP16-032)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Boa Hancock", cost 7, power 9000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[Unblockable] (This card cannot be blocked.) [On Play] Up to 1 of your opponent's Characters other than [Monkey.D.Luffy] cannot be rested until the end of your opponent's next End Phase." CHARACTER. Impel Down/The Seven Warlords of the Sea/Kuja Pirates. OP16-032.]

**Role:** Boss Card, Stall Tool, Finisher

As the highest-cost Impel Down character in the deck, **7C Boa Hancock** is typically the solitary boss card run in Blue-Green Luffy. She offers a 9k body, features the unblockable keyword, and freezes an opposing character on play.

- **Applying a Clock:** If you have established early-to-mid-game pressure and forced cards out of your opponent's hand, dropping **7C Boa Hancock** puts them on a strict timer. Because her attacks cannot be blocked, she is highly likely to take a Life card every single turn she remains on the field, while simultaneously disabling an opposing unit from contesting the board on play.
- **Neutralizing High-Value Targets:** Her On Play effect prevents a chosen character from resting until the end of your opponent's next turn. This means she stops threats from attacking and completely neutralizes blockers. You can use this to disable defensive walls, such as **6C Rosi** or **10C Newgate**, clearing the path for your smaller characters to push damage safely.
- **Control Event Layering:** Thanks to your Leader ability granting 2 extra active DON!!, you can easily deploy **7C Boa Hancock** and layer additional control options on the exact same turn. This allows you to combine her with cards like **4C Galdino, 3C Electrical Luna,** or **1C All Out** to freeze multiple opposing threats simultaneously.
- **Lethal Synergies:** She pairs perfectly with your late-game restand mechanics, specifically **6C Brook** and **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters**. Forcing two unblockable 9k base swings into the opponent in a single turn often secures immediate lethal or sets up a guaranteed win on the following turn.
- **Statline Efficiency:** Her 9k base power shines against self-buffing Leaders like Red-Blue Ace or Blue-Yellow Nami, allowing her to threaten their high power thresholds without requiring heavy DON!! investment.

**Recommendation:** Run 2 to 3 copies. Because she lacks a counter value and acts as a brick in the early game, running 4 copies is risky. This specific ratio ensures you consistently draw into at least one copy by the late game without flooding your opening hands with counterless cards.

### 6C Brook (OP15-032)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Brook", cost 6, power 6000, #COUNTER +1000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[On Play] Rest up to 1 of your opponent's cards. [Activate: Main] You may trash this Character: If your Leader has the [Straw Hat Crew] type, set up to 1 of your Characters with a base cost of 8 or less as active." CHARACTER. Straw Hat Crew. OP15-032.]

**Role**: Late-Game Lethal Finisher

**6C Brook** functions as a primary late-game finisher. While he lacks the Impel Down trait, meaning you cannot trigger your Leader ability while he remains on the board, this restriction is irrelevant because you trash him immediately upon deployment to activate his restand effect.

- **Leader Restand Interaction:** Because you play him and immediately trash him to restand a character, the field remains clear of non-Impel Down units. This allows you to freely use your Leader ability right afterward to restand 2 DON!! and attach them directly to your newly restand target.
- **Denying Counter Events:** His On Play effect can rest any card on the board, not just characters. Aside from clearing blockers out of the way to ensure your attacks connect, you can use Brook to rest your opponent's active DON!!, stopping them from activating defensive counter events.
- **The Double Unblockable Setup:** Brook is most effective when used to restand **7C Boa Hancock** after her initial attack, forcing two unblockable swings in a single turn. When your opponent's hand and Life are low, you can execute this exact sequence:
  - Attach 4 DON!! to **7C Boa Hancock** and make an unblockable attack into the opposing Leader for 13k power.
  - Play **6C Brook** and trash him to restand your **7C Boa Hancock**.
  - Activate your Leader ability to restand 2 DON!!, attach them to **7C Boa Hancock**, and make a second unblockable swing for 15k power.
- **Flexible Alternative Targets:** If you do not have **7C Boa Hancock** on board, Brook can restand any other unit to push for lethal. Ideal alternatives include your 6k power characters boosted by **2C Galdino**, or a **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** scaling against high-power Leaders like Blue-Yellow Nami.
- **Delayed Setup Value:** In niche scenarios where you want to secure lethal for the subsequent turn, you can play **6C Brook** as your final action after using your Leader effect. Leaving him on the board allows you to attack with his 6k body on the following turn before trashing him to restand another threat.

**Recommendation:** Run 2 copies. This card consistently secures late-game wins by catching opponents off guard, and having it in hand allows you to easily map out your final offensive turns.

### 1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters (OP16-038)

> [IMMAGINE: Event card art "Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters!!", cost 1, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[Main] You may rest 6 of your DON!! cards: If you have 5 [Impel Down] type Characters with different card names, set your Leader and all of your Characters as active. [Counter] Your Leader gains +3000 power during this battle." EVENT. Impel Down/Straw Hat Crew. OP16-038.]

**Role**: Searchable Core Win Condition, Lethal Combo Finisher, Defensive 3k Counter

Commonly referred to as the board restand event, **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** serves as the primary win condition for this deck. If you control 5 Impel Down characters with distinct names, this event restands your Leader and your entire character board after they have already swung. This enables a massive offensive turn of up to 12 total attacks.

- **Closing the Game:** Resolving this card in the late game when your opponent is down to 1 or 2 Life and low on hand resources generally wins the game on the spot. While meeting the 5 unique names requirement might seem difficult on paper, it is highly achievable due to your Leader's 2 active DON!! generation, your hand-cycling low-cost units, and chain-deployment engines like **5C Buggy** and **4C Ivankov**.
- **Calculated Patience:** Playing around this card effectively requires disciplined board management. If you see a clear path to winning with the restand event in the coming turns, you must protect your units. Avoid swinging recklessly into opposing threats; keeping your characters alive to satisfy the activation condition is far more important than forcing early, non-lethal attacks.
- **Searchability and Planning:** Because the event is entirely searchable, you can grab it early and map out your late-game turns ahead of time. If your opponent manages to dismantle your board and prevents you from keeping 5 unique names active, the card shifts into an emergency 3k counter value to protect your Leader while you transition into a defensive strategy using blockers and freeze effects.

**Recommendation:** Run 4 copies for maximum consistency, especially to outpace opponents in mirror matches. However, this count can be adjusted based on playstyle; dropping down to 2 copies is perfectly viable if you prefer a heavier control approach utilizing cards like **1C All Out** or **3C Electrical Luna**.

### 4C Rush Luffy (ST30-012)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Monkey.D.Luffy", cost 4, power 6000. Text: "[On Play] You may rest 1 of your DON!! cards: This Character gains [Rush] this turn. (This card can attack on the turn in which it is played.) [When Attacking] Rest up to 1 of your opponent's Characters with [Blocker]." CHARACTER. Impel Down / Straw Hat Crew. ST30-012.]

**Role**: Late-Game Combo Finisher, Mid-Game Rest-and-Rush Utility

**4C Rush Luffy** is a standout Green option due to how uncommon the Rush keyword is for the color. While he requires a total investment of 5 DON!! to gain Rush, he remains flexible; if your early turn setup is weak, you can simply deploy him as a standard vanilla character on your 4 DON!! turn.

- **Bypassing Blocker Walls:** His primary utility lies in his ability to rest an opposing blocker whenever he attacks. In the late game, this turns him into an effective finisher that clears defensive lines while adding an extra immediate attacker to your push.
- **Neutralizing On-KO Threats:** In the mid-game, this rest effect allows you to bypass blocker units with disruptive On-KO abilities, such as Red-Blue Ace's **4C Marco** or Red Shanks's **4C Lucky Roo**, preventing your opponent from gaining value from them blocking.
- **Managing Counterless Slots:** The main drawback to running this card is its lack of a counter value. Flooding your deck with counterless units can severely compromise your defense during your opponent's turn.

**Recommendation:** Run 1 to 2 copies. Alternatively, you can omit him entirely if you prefer to lower your counterless card count, provided your deck already runs sufficient numbers of **6C Brook** or **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** to consistently draw into your late-game finishers.

## 2k Counters

> [IMMAGINE: Two-card row on a green/blue radar background. Card 1: cost 2, power 2000, "Jinbe". Card 2: cost 2, power 3000, "Mr.3(Galdino)".]

While your primary 2k counter value comes from running 8 copies of **6C Prisoners of Impel Down** due to its specific synergy with **5C Buggy**, relying solely on them limits your flexibility. To consistently fulfill the unique-name activation requirements of your **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** restand turns, it is necessary to supplement your list with low-cost utility 2k counters. These units add useful field effects to your strategy while expanding your pool of distinct character names.

### 2C Galdino 2k (ST30-014)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Mr.3(Galdino)", cost 2, power 3000, #COUNTER +2000. Text: "[Activate: Main] You may rest this Character: Give up to 2 of your Characters with a base power of 6000 up to 2 rested DON!! card each." CHARACTER. Impel Down/Former Baroque Works. ST30-014.]

**Role**: Stat-Boosting Combo Piece, Emergency Restand Enabler

**2C Galdino 2k** acts as an efficient offensive booster, optimizing your DON!! allocation when you need to push heavy damage into your opponent.

- **DON!! Multiplication Value:** Once on the board, he can rest himself to attach 2 rested DON!! cards each to two of your characters that have a base power of 6k. By investing 2 active DON!! to play him, his effect extracts a total of 4 rested DON!! from your resource pool, providing excellent value. This is effective when pushing for lethal with two 6k bodies on your board, or when you need to inflate your characters' power to clear opposing characters.
- **Restand and Bounce Loops:** If you activate his effect and rest him, resolving your **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** restand event will stand him back up, allowing you to trigger his DON!!-attachment ability a second time in the same turn. Additionally, you can utilize **4C Crocodile** to bounce and replay him, resetting his activation state entirely.
- **Overcoming High-Power Leaders:** This card shines against Leaders with elevated base power thresholds, allowing your mid-size units to swing for high damage without draining your active DON!! resources.

### 2C Jinbe 2k (OP16-027)

> [IMMAGINE: Card art of "Jinbe", cost 2, power 2000, #COUNTER +2000, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[DON!!x1] This Character gains +2000 power." CHARACTER. Fish-Man/Impel Down/The Sun Pirates. OP16-027.]

**Role**: Emergency Low-Cost Attacker, Resilient Early-Game Unit

**2C Jinbe 2k** provides solid defensive resilience for a low-cost unit. His text grants him +2k power as long as he has at least 1 DON!! attached to him, which effectively shifts him from a 2k base body into a 4k power character.

- **Persistent Power Scaling:** A key aspect of his ability is that the +2k power buff acts as a passive effect that remains active during your opponent's turn. Because he sits at 4k power on defense with a single DON!! attached, your opponent cannot use their small characters to pick him off for free. They are forced to commit an attack of 5k power or greater just to clear him from the field.
- **Early-Game Flexibility:** This defensive statline makes him a reliable backup option to develop on your early turns if you fail to find **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay** or **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones**. In a worst-case scenario, he simply functions as a standard 2k defensive counter from your hand, or as a cheap body to help meet the unique-name requirement for your **1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters** combo.

**Recommendation:** Running a split of 2 copies of **2C Galdino 2k** and 2 copies of **2C Jinbe 2k** provides a balanced pool of distinct names. You can adjust this ratio based on your playstyle: lean into more copies of **2C Galdino 2k** for explosive offensive turns, or increase your count of **2C Jinbe 2k** to improve your early-game board-swarming consistency.

## Consistency Techs

> [IMMAGINE: Three card proxies on a green/blue radar background. Left: cost 0, Gum Gum Rain. Middle: cost 1, Dead Man's Game (green event). Right: cost 5, power 6000, Miss Olive.]

These cards are included to make the deck more reliable. They provide ways to filter unplayable, counterless cards from your hand, handle blocker-heavy boards, or find specific combo pieces when your standard draws are missing.

### 0C Gum-Gum Rain (OP02-068)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Gum Gum Rain (OP02-068), cost 0, blue Event, "RAIN!!!" art. Text: "[Counter] You may trash 1 card from your hand: Your Leader or up to 1 of your Characters gains +3000 Power during this battle. [Trigger] Return up to 1 Character with a cost of 2 or less to the owner's hand." Type line: Impel Down / Straw Hat Pirates.]

**Role**: Counterless Card Discarder, Leader and Character Defense

**0C Gum-Gum Rain** is a 0-cost 3k counter event that lets you discard unplayable, counterless cards from your hand to generate defensive value in the late game.

- **Hand Filtering:** This deck runs several counterless cards like **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay**, **7C Boa Hancock**, and **4C Crocodile**. Because of this, you will frequently end up with 1 or 2 unplayable cards stuck in your hand. This event allows you to convert those dead resources into immediate protection.
- **Flexible Target Protection:** Most of the deck's other 3k counter events (**1C Let's Go!! To the Navy Headquarters**, **1C Dead Man's Game**, and **1C All Out**) are strictly restricted to protecting your Leader. **0C Gum-Gum Rain** can be used to protect your characters as well, which helps keep your key board pieces alive.

**Recommendation:** Run 0 to 2 copies, depending on the total number of counterless cards included in your specific list.

### 1C Dead Man's Game (OP12-037)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Demonic Aura Nine-Sword Style Asura Dead Man's Game (OP12-037), cost 1, green Event, "DEAD MAN'S GAME!!" art (Zoro). Text: "[Main] You may rest 3 of your DON!! cards: Rest up to a total of 2 of your opponent's Characters or DON!! cards. [Counter] Up to 1 of your Leader gains +3000 power during this battle." Type line: Straw Hat Crew.]

**Role**: Lethal Combo Finisher, 3k Leader Counter

**1C Dead Man's Game** provides strong utility by clearing out blockers to pave the way for lethal. In the worst case scenario, it simply acts as a 3k counter event for your Leader.

- **Bypassing Blocker-Heavy Boards:** The meta relies heavily on blockers, such as Purple Enel running **4C Reiju** and **4C Franky**, or Purple-Yellow Rosinante deploying multiple **Rosinante** and **Trafalgar Law** blockers. This card rests these defenders to secure late-game wins. Because your Leader ability gives you 2 extra active DON!!, you can resolve this event and still have 8 active DON!! remaining to push your attacks.
- **Disrupting Counter Events:** Resting your opponent's active DON!! cards with this event stops them from playing defensive counter options, such as the mirror match events or Purple-Yellow Rosinante's **1C I love you!**.
- **Board Control and Setup:** Beyond going for game, you can use the rest effect to clear the opponent's characters through battle, or set them up to be frozen on the following turn by **3C Electrical Luna** or **1C All Out**.

**Recommendation:** Run 0 to 2 copies, depending on how consistently you want to draw into this late-game finishing option.

### 5C Miss Olive (OP16-050)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Miss Olive (OP16-050), cost 5, power 6000, #COUNTER +1000, blue Character, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[Blocker] [On Play] You may return 1 of your Characters with a cost of 2 or more to the owner's hand: Draw 2 cards and trash 1 card from your hand." Type line: Impel Down.]

**Role**: Hand Filter, Card Advantage, Emergency Blocker

**5C Miss Olive** generates significant hand advantage through her on-play filtering effect. She requires you to bounce one of your own characters to your hand to trigger a draw-two, discard-one sequence.

- **Targeted Bouncing:** Your primary targets for her bounce effect are **2C Mr. 1 Daz Bones** and **2C Mr. 2 Bon Clay**, allowing you to replay them later for more card draw. Alternatively, you can bounce your 2k counters back to hand if you need immediate defensive counter power.
- **Deck Digging and Filtering:** Her draw-two, trash-one effect is her most valuable trait. It lets you dig deep into your deck for key combo pieces while safely discarding counterless bricks that are unplayable in the current game state.
- **Defensive Utility:** Beyond filtering your hand, she remains on the board as a blocker to absorb incoming attacks during your opponent's turn.
- **Distinct from 4C Crocodile:** While frequently compared to **4C Crocodile**, the two cards serve completely different roles. Crocodile bounces and immediately replays your 2c targets onto the field to maintain board presence and draw cards. Miss Olive only returns the target to your hand, meaning you must invest resources to play them manually again. Crocodile focuses on maintaining board pressure while developing defense, whereas Miss Olive is used purely for deck digging and brick filtering.

**Recommendation:** Run 0 to 2 copies. She is strong but can sometimes be difficult to fit into your optimal turn curves. Include her based on personal preference if you want extra card filtering and draw power.

## Control Techs

> [IMMAGINE: Four card proxies on a green/blue radar background. Left: cost 1, I Know You're Strong...So I'll Go All Out From the Start!!! Second: cost 3, Electrical Luna. Third: cost 7, Gravity Blade Raging Tiger. Right: cost 3, Gum-Gum Jet Culverin.]

Using control events traditionally causes a loss in tempo because you spend resources managing the opponent's board instead of developing characters. However, because your Leader ability generates 2 extra active DON!! every turn, you can frequently play these control cards to disrupt your opponent while still maintaining enough resources to develop your own board.

### 1C All Out (OP13-040)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — I Know You're Strong...So I'll Go All Out From the Start!!! (OP13-040), cost 1, green Event, Gear art (Luffy). Text: "[Main] You may rest 2 of your DON!! cards: Up to 2 of your opponent's rested Characters with a cost of 7 or less will not become active in your opponent's next Refresh Phase. [Counter] Up to 1 of your Leader gains +3000 power during this battle." Type line: Supernovas/Straw Hat Crew.]

**Role**: Anti-Aggro and Midrange Stall Tool, 3k Leader Counter

**1C All Out** is a stable defensive option for Green that remains reliable in the OP16 meta. It focuses on freezing opposing threats to stall out your opponent's offensive turns.

- **Freezing Midrange Threats:** The ability to freeze two midrange characters helps disrupt opposing strategies. It is effective for stunning key units against Purple Enel and Purple-Yellow Rosinante, and it serves as a valuable tool in the Blue-Green Luffy mirror match to help you win the damage race.
- **Defensive Flexibility:** In the worst-case scenario where you do not need the board control effect, the card can always be used as a 3k counter to protect your Leader.

**Recommendation:** A safe and reliable tech choice for open fields.

### 3C Electrical Luna (OP08-036)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Electrical Luna (OP08-036), cost 3, green Event, "BLZZAAAP!!!" art. Text: "[Main] All of your opponent's rested characters with a cost of 7 or less do not become active during your opponent's next refresh phase. [Trigger] Rest up to one of your opponent's characters." Type line: Minks.]

**Role**: High-Ceiling Surprise Control Tool

**3C Electrical Luna** serves as a high-reward alternative to **1C All Out**, offering a significantly higher ceiling by providing the potential to freeze your opponent's entire board.

- **Mass Board Freeze:** The main effect can stall multiple threats simultaneously. It synergizes exceptionally well with your other freeze effects, such as **7C Boa Hancock** and **4C Galdino**, allowing you to lock down three or more characters at once to completely halt an opponent's momentum.
- **Blowout Trigger:** Its Trigger offers defensive swing potential. When taken from your Life cards, it can rest any character regardless of its cost or power level, effectively shutting down your opponent's lethal pushes.
- **The Counterless Drawback:** The primary risk of running this card is that it lacks a counter value. If you draw into it in a matchup where its effect isn't needed, it becomes a dead card in your hand, exacerbating the deck's existing vulnerability to bloating with counterless bricks.

**Recommendation:** Run this if you want a higher-ceiling blowout card to swing specific meta matchups, but ensure you carefully balance your overall counterless card count. Stick to **1C All Out** if you prefer a lower-risk option that is never entirely dead in hand.

### 7C Gravity Blade (OP06-058)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Gravity Blade Raging Tiger (OP06-058), cost 7, blue Event, city-with-crossed-swords art. Text: "[Main] Place up to 2 cost 6 or less characters in any order on the bottom of their owner's deck. [Trigger] Place up to one cost 5 or less character on the bottom of its owner's deck." Type line: Navy.]

**Role**: Lead-Closer, Surprise Midrange Board Removal

**7C Gravity Blade Raging Tiger** is a powerful Blue removal event that places up to two opposing characters with a cost of 6 or less to the bottom of the owner's deck. This effectively clears out most threats developed during the early to mid-game turns.

- **Capitalizing on Board Presence:** This card is most effective when you already have established threats on the field. Because this deck can quickly swarm the board with 3 or 4 characters, casting this event on your 7 or 8 DON!! turn creates a tempo swing. If the game is even or you are already ahead, resolving this card often seals the win.
- **Bypassing On-KO Triggers:** Sending characters to the bottom of the deck lets you completely bypass annoying On-KO effects. This is useful against Blue-Yellow Nami (**5C Nami** or **4C Kiku**), Red-Blue Ace (**4C Marco**), and Red Shanks (**4C Lucky Roo**).
- **Impactful Life Trigger:** Its Trigger can bottom deck an opposing attacker instantly without requiring you to spend any DON!! resources on your turn.
- **Resource Cost and Dead Draws:** The main drawbacks are its high 7-DON!! cost and its lack of a counter value. If you cast this while your own board is empty, you spend almost your entire turn removing threats instead of developing your own win conditions. If drawn in the wrong matchup, it becomes an unplayable brick in hand.

**Recommendation:** Run a maximum of 1 copy unless the meta strictly demands more, as you will rarely realistically cast more than one per game. It shines brightest in the mirror match, or against Red Ace, Black-Yellow Teach, and Purple-Yellow Rosinante.

### 3C Jet Culverin (OP11-061)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Gum-Gum Jet Culverin (OP11-061), cost 3, blue Event, "JET" art. Text: "[Main] Return up to 1 of your opponent's Characters with an original cost of 4 or less to the bottom of their deck. [Trigger] Return up to 1 of your opponent's Characters with a cost of 1 or less to the bottom of their deck." Type line: Straw Hat Crew.]

**Role**: Targeted Tech Choice, Mid-Game Tempo Removal

**3C Jet Culverin** acts as a downscaled, more resource-efficient version of **7C Gravity Blade**, allowing you to place an opposing character with a base cost of 4 or less to the bottom of the deck.

- **Targeted Matchup Utility:** This card is primarily a tech option used to target Purple Enel, as it directly removes core mid-game pieces like **3C Holly** and **4C Vinsmoke Reiju**.
- **Maintaining Development Tempo:** Because it costs only 3 DON!! to cast, your Leader's ability to refresh 2 active DON!! means you can resolve this removal event while still retaining enough resources to develop characters and progress your own board state on the same turn.
- **The Brick Risk:** Its utility is highly matchup-dependent. Against strategies that do not rely on targets with a cost of 4 or less, this card lacks a counter value and can easily get stuck in your hand as an unplayable brick.

**Recommendation:** Run 0 to 2 copies, scaling your count strictly based on the amount of Purple Enel you expect to face in your specific tournament meta.

### 2C Galdino Rest (OP16-037)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Mr.3(Galdino) (OP16-037), cost 2, power 3000, #COUNTER +1000, green Character, watermarked SAMPLE. Text: "[On Play] If your Leader has the [Impel Down] type, rest up to 1 of your opponent's Characters with a cost of 5 or less." Type line: Impel Down/Former Baroque Works.]

**Role**: Early-Game Board Control, Freeze Event Enabler

**2C Galdino** is a tech option that forces an opponent's character to rest, giving you immediate tactical flexibility on the board.

- **Forced Resting for Combat or Lockdowns:** Resting an opposing character allows you to either attack directly into them to clear them by battle, or leave them vulnerable to be frozen on the following turn by your freeze events, such as **3C Electrical Luna** or **1C All Out**.
- **Crocodile Bounce Loops:** You can use **4C Crocodile** to bounce this card back to your hand and replay it on the same turn. This resets his on-play effect, allowing you to rest a second opposing character and systematically clear out their field.
- **Winning the Early-Game Board Race:** This card is effective at winning early battles against small characters, giving you a distinct advantage in the Purple Enel matchup and the Blue-Green Luffy mirror.

**Recommendation:** Run this card if you need an efficient early-game tool to combat aggressive low-cost boards or setup your mid-game freeze packages.

### 6C Crocodile (ST30-010)

> [IMMAGINE: Card proxy — Crocodile (ST30-010), cost 6, power 6000, #COUNTER +1000, green Character. **Role**: Dual-Purpose Stall Tool, Defensive Blocker

**6C Crocodile** is a defensive tech option that combines a board-control freeze effect with a defensive blocker body.

- **Two-in-One Attack Denial:** Upon being played, this card freezes an opponent's rested character while establishing a 6k power blocker on your own field. This allows you to potentially deny two incoming attacks late in the game with a single card investment, which can be useful in specific defensive, fortress-style scenarios.
- **Low Stat Efficiency:** The main drawback of this card is its cost-to-power ratio. A 6k power statline is quite low for a 6-cost character in the competitive meta. Because it lacks high offensive or defensive impact on its own, it often feels mediocre when compared to the deck's more powerful and cost-effective tech alternatives.

**Recommendation:** Generally omitted or run at 0 copies, as other tech options offer significantly higher value for their cost.
